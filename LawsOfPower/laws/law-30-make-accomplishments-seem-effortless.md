# Law 30 — Make Your Accomplishments Seem Effortless

**The Law:** Your actions must seem natural and executed with ease. All the toil and practice that go
into them, and also all the clever tricks, must be concealed. When you act, act effortlessly, as if you
could do much more. Avoid the temptation of revealing how hard you work — it only raises questions.
Teach no one your tricks or they will be used against you.

**Essence:** Visible effort cheapens the achievement; effortless mastery seems like destiny. Hide the
sweat and the tricks, and your power appears natural, limitless, almost magical.

**How a character enacts it:** The figure of seemingly effortless mastery hides the sleepless nights and
the mechanics behind the trick, presenting only the flawless result with a careless ease. They never
explain how it's done — mystery preserves the power, and a revealed method is a method that can be stolen
or countered. They make the impossible look like a shrug.

**Tells, tactics & dialogue:** *"Oh, that? It was nothing."* (after weeks of secret labor). The
magician who never explains the trick. A character whose talents seem to flow without effort, leaving
rivals demoralized and audiences awed.

**Counter & backfire:** Concealed effort means concealed cost — and a character who makes everything look
easy may find their work undervalued and underpaid, the difficulty invisible. The mystery, once pierced
(a rival learns the trick), collapses the aura entirely. And feigned ease can read as arrogance,
provoking the envy of Law 46 in those who resent how easy it all looks for them.
