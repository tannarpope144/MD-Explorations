# Law 3 — Conceal Your Intentions

**The Law:** Keep people off balance and in the dark by never revealing the purpose behind
your actions. If they have no clue what you are up to, they cannot prepare a defense. Guide
them far enough down the wrong path, envelop them in enough smoke, and by the time they
realize your aim it will be too late.

**Essence:** Disclosed intent invites resistance. A hidden aim, masked behind a false one,
lets you reach the goal before anyone thinks to block it.

**How a character enacts it:** The schemer broadcasts a decoy desire — feigning interest in
the lesser prize while maneuvering toward the real one. They give plausible, mundane reasons
for moves that secretly serve a single design. Misdirection: act warm toward the very person
you intend to ruin. Let observers think you're sentimental, distracted, or after the obvious
thing.

**Tells, tactics & dialogue:** Stated reasons that don't quite add up in hindsight. *"Oh, I
have no interest in the northern estate"* — said by the one quietly buying its debts. A
character whose explained motive is always one layer shallower than their real one.

**Counter & backfire:** A sharp observer notices the *pattern* of misdirection and stops
believing anything the schemer says — total loss of credibility (Law 5). Over-concealment can
also confuse allies who'd have helped, leaving the schemer to do alone what a candid word
would have shared. And the smoke can blind the schemer themselves to a simpler, honest path.
