# Law 27 — Play on People's Need to Believe to Create a Cultlike Following

**The Law:** People have an overwhelming desire to believe in something. Become the focal point of
such desire by offering them a cause, a new faith to follow. Keep your words vague but full of promise;
emphasize enthusiasm over rationality and clear thinking. Give your new disciples rituals to perform,
ask them to make sacrifices on your behalf.

**Essence:** People hunger for meaning and belonging. The figure who offers a cause and a faith can
command devotion, sacrifice, and blind loyalty that reason could never buy.

**How a character enacts it:** The demagogue or cult-leader offers a grand, vague promise — salvation,
glory, belonging — and stokes fervor over scrutiny. They create rituals and us-versus-them enemies to
bind the flock, demand sacrifices that deepen commitment, and position themselves as the indispensable
prophet. The vaguer the promise, the more each follower fills it with their own longing.

**Tells, tactics & dialogue:** Soaring, unfalsifiable promises. *"Together we will build something the
world has never seen — believe, and you will be part of it."* Manufactured enemies, loyalty tests,
ecstatic crowds. A character who makes followers feel *chosen*.

**Counter & backfire:** Belief curdles into fanaticism the leader can no longer control — the mob turns
on its prophet the instant the promise visibly fails. A single skeptic who punctures the vagueness with
a hard question can break the spell. And the leader who starts believing their own myth loses the cold
calculation that built it, undone by the faith they manufactured.
