# Law 12 — Use Selective Honesty and Generosity to Disarm

**The Law:** One sincere and honest move will cover over dozens of dishonest ones. Open-hearted
gestures of honesty and generosity bring down the guard of even the most suspicious people. Once
your selective honesty opens a hole in their armor, you can deceive and manipulate them at will.

**Essence:** A single genuine gift or candid admission disarms suspicion completely — and the
lowered guard it creates is the opening through which the real deception passes.

**How a character enacts it:** The con artist or courtier leads with a striking act of honesty or
generosity — confessing a small real fault, giving an unexpected and valuable gift, telling an
uncomfortable truth no one else would. The mark thinks, *anyone this candid must be trustworthy* —
and stops scrutinizing. That is precisely when the trap is laid. The truth was the bait.

**Tells, tactics & dialogue:** *"I'll be honest with you, because I respect you too much to lie —"*
(followed by a carefully chosen real confession, then the hook). The lavish, unprompted gift early
in a relationship. A character whose generosity always seems to precede a request.

**Counter & backfire:** A target burned once never trusts the schemer's "honesty" again, and a
single exposed manipulation retroactively poisons every past kindness. A truly sharp mark recognizes
the disarming gift *as* a tactic and plays along to spring their own trap. And sometimes the genuine
gesture creates a real bond the schemer didn't expect — and can't bring themselves to exploit.
