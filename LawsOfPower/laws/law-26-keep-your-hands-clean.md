# Law 26 — Keep Your Hands Clean

**The Law:** You must seem a paragon of civility and efficiency: your hands are never soiled by
mistakes and nasty deeds. Maintain such a spotless appearance by using others as scapegoats and
cat's-paws to disguise your involvement. Conceal your dirty work behind others' hands.

**Essence:** The powerful never hold the knife themselves. Let others do the ugly deeds and absorb the
blame, while you keep an unblemished public face.

**How a character enacts it:** The cunning manipulator works through cat's-paws — proxies who carry out
the dirty work believing it's their own idea — and keeps scapegoats ready to absorb any blame. When a
scheme is exposed, someone else takes the fall; the true author is mourning beside the victim. They
never sign their name to anything that could stain them.

**Tells, tactics & dialogue:** *"I had nothing to do with it — it was his doing entirely."* (perfectly
true on paper). The trusted lieutenant who "acted alone." A character who is always, conveniently,
somewhere else when the deed is done.

**Counter & backfire:** Cat's-paws have minds of their own — a scapegoat who refuses to take the fall,
or who kept evidence, can expose the master with a word. Use proxies too often and observers start
noticing the pattern of disasters that orbit one suspiciously clean person (Law 3's backfire). And a
discarded cat's-paw who realizes they were used becomes a vengeful witness who knows everything.
