# Law 40 — Despise the Free Lunch

**The Law:** What is offered for free is dangerous — it usually involves either a trick or a hidden
obligation. What has worth is worth paying for. By paying your own way you stay clear of gratitude, guilt,
and deceit. It is often wise to pay full price — there is no cutting corners with excellence. Be lavish
with your money and keep it circulating, for generosity is a sign and a magnet for power.

**Essence:** Nothing is truly free — the gift carries a hook of obligation, the bargain hides a trap. The
shrewd suspect generosity and use their own, lavishly, as a tool of influence.

**How a character enacts it:** The wary character refuses gifts that come with invisible strings, knowing
a debt of gratitude is a leash. Conversely, they wield *strategic* generosity — giving lavishly to bind
others to them, to project power, to buy loyalty that masquerades as friendship. They read every free
offer for the hook and every gift they give as an investment.

**Tells, tactics & dialogue:** *"And what, exactly, do you expect in return?"* The refusal of a too-generous
offer. The character whose own lavish gifts always seem to create a quiet obligation in the receiver.

**Counter & backfire:** Suspicion of every gift makes a character cold and isolated, rejecting genuine
kindness and the allies it would have won. A manipulator can exploit the *knowledge* that the character
distrusts free things by making a trap look expensive and "earned." And generosity given purely to obligate
is sniffed out by the proud, who resent being bought.
