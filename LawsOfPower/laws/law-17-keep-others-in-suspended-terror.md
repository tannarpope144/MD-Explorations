# Law 17 — Keep Others in Suspended Terror: Cultivate an Air of Unpredictability

**The Law:** Humans are creatures of habit with an insatiable need to see familiarity in other
people's actions. Your predictability gives them a sense of control. Turn the tables: be deliberately
unpredictable. Behavior that seems to have no consistency or purpose will keep them off balance, and
they will wear themselves out trying to explain your moves.

**Essence:** Predictability hands others control; calculated unpredictability keeps everyone anxious,
guessing, and unable to plan against you.

**How a character enacts it:** The volatile power-holder mixes generosity with sudden coldness,
reward with arbitrary punishment, so no one can ever feel safe or read what comes next. Subordinates
tiptoe, perpetually trying to please an unreadable master. The occasional inexplicable, dramatic act
keeps the air charged with low-grade fear. Control through instability.

**Tells, tactics & dialogue:** A leader who praises lavishly one day and freezes someone out the next,
for no clear reason. *"No one ever knew which version of him would walk through the door."* The strategic
tantrum or sudden, surprising mercy.

**Counter & backfire:** Constant unpredictability is exhausting and isolating — it breeds not loyalty
but the quiet, unanimous desire to be rid of the tyrant. People stop trying to please an unreadable
master and start conspiring against them. And true randomness can read as madness or weakness rather than
power, inviting a challenge from someone who senses the chaos is fear, not control.
