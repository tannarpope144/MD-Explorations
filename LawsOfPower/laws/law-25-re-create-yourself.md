# Law 25 — Re-Create Yourself

**The Law:** Do not accept the roles that society foists on you. Re-create yourself by forging a new
identity, one that commands attention and never bores the audience. Be the master of your own image
rather than letting others define it for you. Incorporate dramatic devices into your public gestures
and actions — your power will be enhanced and your character will seem larger than life.

**Essence:** Identity is not fixed but forged. The figure who deliberately authors their own myth and
image controls how the world sees and treats them.

**How a character enacts it:** The self-made figure sheds the role they were born into and builds a new
one — a new name, a new manner, a crafted persona larger than life. They control their image like a
performance: every public gesture is theater, every appearance reinforces the legend. The lowborn who
remakes themselves into nobility; the unremarkable person who becomes an icon by sheer force of
self-invention.

**Tells, tactics & dialogue:** *"I am not the man I was born as. I am the man I made."* A character with
a carefully constructed origin story and a persona that doesn't quite match their roots. Deliberate,
theatrical self-presentation.

**Counter & backfire:** A forged identity has a true self underneath — and the threat of exposure
(someone who knew them "before") haunts the self-made character and invites blackmail. Re-invent too
often and the persona rings hollow, a mask with nothing behind it. The strain of maintaining a self
that isn't real can fracture the character from within — who are they when the performance stops?
