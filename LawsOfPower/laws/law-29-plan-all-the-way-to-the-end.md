# Law 29 — Plan All the Way to the End

**The Law:** The ending is everything. Plan all the way to it, taking into account all the possible
consequences, obstacles, and twists of fortune that might reverse your hard work and give the glory to
others. By planning to the end you will not be overwhelmed by circumstances and you will know when to
stop. Gently guide fortune and help determine the future by thinking far ahead.

**Essence:** The masters of power see the whole game before the first move. Foreseeing every
consequence and twist, they steer events while lesser players merely react.

**How a character enacts it:** The grand strategist maps the entire arc — every contingency, every
rival's likely response, every way fortune could turn — before acting. They set traps that won't spring
for years and aren't surprised by "twists" because they foresaw them. Their apparent luck is foresight;
their calm in chaos comes from having already planned for it.

**Tells, tactics & dialogue:** *"I planned for this the day it began."* The reveal that a move made
long ago was preparing for this exact moment. A character three steps ahead of everyone, including the
reader.

**Counter & backfire:** No plan survives contact with a truly chaotic world — the over-planner is
brittle when reality serves up something they didn't foresee, frozen because the script broke (the
counter to this is Law 48, formlessness). Obsessive planning can also become paralysis. And a cunning
rival who *learns* the schemer plans far ahead can feed them a false future to plan around.
