# Law 37 — Create Compelling Spectacles

**The Law:** Striking imagery and grand symbolic gestures create the aura of power — everyone responds
to them. Stage spectacles for those around you, then, full of arresting visuals and radiant symbols that
heighten your presence. Dazzled by appearances, no one will notice what you are really doing.

**Essence:** A grand spectacle commands belief and emotion that argument never could — and while every
eye is dazzled by the show, the real work happens unwatched.

**How a character enacts it:** The showman-ruler stages dazzling displays — ceremonies, symbols, dramatic
public gestures — that overwhelm reason and bind the audience emotionally. The spectacle does double duty:
it cements their image *and* distracts from what they're quietly doing offstage. They understand that
people remember the image, not the substance.

**Tells, tactics & dialogue:** The grand entrance, the symbolic gift, the staged public moment timed for
maximum effect. *"Give them a spectacle they'll never forget — they won't think to ask what it cost."* A
character who governs through theater.

**Counter & backfire:** Spectacle is expensive and hollow if the substance behind it is exposed — a single
person who looks past the dazzle to the truth can break the spell. Escalating spectacles must grow ever
grander to keep their effect, a ruinous arms race. And a disaster *during* the grand display — the
ceremony that goes wrong before every eye — magnifies humiliation as powerfully as it would have magnified
glory.
