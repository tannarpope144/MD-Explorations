# Law 13 — Appeal to Self-Interest, Never to Mercy or Gratitude

**The Law:** If you need to turn to an ally for help, do not bother to remind them of your past
assistance and good deeds. They will find a way to ignore you. Instead, uncover something in your
request, or in your alliance with them, that will benefit them, and emphasize it out of all
proportion. They will respond enthusiastically when they see something to be gained.

**Essence:** People are moved by what they stand to gain, not by guilt or the memory of favors.
Frame every request as the other person's opportunity.

**How a character enacts it:** The persuader never says *"you owe me."* They study the other's
greed, fear, or ambition and frame the request as the path to satisfying it: *"Help me, and here's
what it puts in your pocket."* They make the ally feel the deal is *their* clever move, not a favor
extracted. Cold reading of self-interest replaces any appeal to sentiment.

**Tells, tactics & dialogue:** *"Forget what I did for you — think about what this does for you."*
The pitch always angled toward the listener's advantage. A character who instinctively reframes "I
need" into "you'll gain."

**Counter & backfire:** Appeal purely to self-interest and you teach everyone that you, too, can be
bought — your allies feel no loyalty, only price, and the higher bidder takes them. Misreading what
someone actually wants makes the whole pitch fall flat. And a person of genuine principle is
*insulted* by the assumption they only move for gain — the appeal backfires into contempt.
