# Law 41 — Avoid Stepping Into a Great Man's Shoes

**The Law:** What happens first always appears better and more original than what comes after. If you
succeed a great man or have a famous parent, you will have to accomplish double their achievement to
outshine them. Do not get lost in their shadow, or stuck in a past not of your own making: establish your
own name and identity by changing course. Slay the overbearing father, disparage his legacy, and gain
power by shining in your own way.

**Essence:** Inheriting a giant's position means being measured forever against them and always coming up
short. To claim real power, escape the shadow — break from the legacy and forge your own.

**How a character enacts it:** The successor refuses to be a mere caretaker of someone else's glory. They
deliberately break from the predecessor — a new direction, a symbolic repudiation, sometimes the public
tearing-down of the old hero's legacy — to claim a name of their own. The heir who burns the father's
playbook; the protégé who must surpass and supplant the mentor.

**Tells, tactics & dialogue:** *"They will not remember me as his shadow. They will remember me."* The
deliberate departure from a revered predecessor's path. A character chafing under the weight of a legacy
they didn't choose.

**Counter & backfire:** Repudiating a beloved predecessor can outrage their loyal followers and brand the
successor an ingrate or a traitor. Changing course just to be different — discarding what genuinely worked
— is self-sabotage. And the harder the heir struggles against the shadow, the more they may define
themselves entirely in opposition to it, never escaping it at all.
