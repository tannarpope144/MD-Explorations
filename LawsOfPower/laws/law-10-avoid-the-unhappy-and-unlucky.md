# Law 10 — Infection: Avoid the Unhappy and Unlucky

**The Law:** You can die from someone else's misery — emotional states are as infectious as
diseases. The unfortunate sometimes draw misfortune on themselves; they will also draw it onto
you. Associate with the happy and fortunate instead.

**Essence:** Misery, chaos, and bad luck spread by contact. The chronically doomed pull everyone
near them down, and proximity makes their downfall yours.

**How a character enacts it:** The self-protective climber quietly distances themselves from the
falling ally, the perpetual victim, the cursed venture — cutting ties before the contagion spreads.
They cultivate the company of winners, whose fortune and confidence rub off. It is cold, often
ruthless: the friend who needs them most is the one they abandon, because need is a sinkhole.

**Tells, tactics & dialogue:** *"I'm sorry, truly — but I can't be pulled into this."* The
sudden cooling toward someone whose luck has turned. The character who's always somehow already
gone before the ship sinks.

**Counter & backfire:** This is how a character earns a reputation as a fair-weather friend — and
when *their* luck turns, they find everyone has learned the same lesson and abandons them in kind.
Mistaking a temporary slump for doom means cutting loose someone who'd have become a powerful ally.
The coldness can also be the moral line that defines them as a villain in the reader's eyes.
