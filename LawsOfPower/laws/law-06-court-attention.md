# Law 6 — Court Attention at All Costs

**The Law:** Everything is judged by its appearance; what is unseen counts for nothing. Never
let yourself get lost in the crowd, then, or buried in oblivion. Stand out. Be conspicuous at
all cost. Make yourself a magnet of attention by appearing larger, more colorful, more
mysterious than the bland and timid masses.

**Essence:** To be talked about is to have power; obscurity is a kind of death. Court controversy
if you must — any attention beats none.

**How a character enacts it:** The attention-seeker manufactures intrigue around themselves — a
striking style, a deliberate air of mystery, a calculated scandal. They'd rather be slandered than
ignored. They attach their name to spectacle, pick visible fights, and ensure the room knows when
they enter and feels it when they leave.

**Tells, tactics & dialogue:** A grand entrance. A cultivated eccentricity. *"Let them talk —
the only thing worse than being talked about is not being talked about."* The character who
engineers a public moment precisely when eyes might drift elsewhere.

**Counter & backfire:** Attention is fickle and devouring — the figure who lives on it must
escalate endlessly to keep it, and the crowd that lifts them up turns just as fast. Courting
scandal can corrode the reputation of Law 5. And a rival can simply *deny* the spotlight (Law 36),
starving the attention-addict of the oxygen they need, watching them flail for relevance.
