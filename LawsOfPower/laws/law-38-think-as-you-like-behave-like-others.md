# Law 38 — Think as You Like, But Behave Like Others

**The Law:** If you make a show of going against the times, flaunting your unconventional ideas and
unorthodox ways, people will think that you only want attention and that you look down upon them. They
will find a way to punish you for making them feel inferior. It is far safer to blend in and nurture the
common touch. Share your originality only with tolerant friends and those who are sure to appreciate it.

**Essence:** Open nonconformity provokes the herd to punish you. Keep your true, heretical thoughts
hidden behind a conventional mask, and move freely among people who'd destroy you if they knew.

**How a character enacts it:** The secret heretic wears the mask of orthodoxy — attending the rituals,
mouthing the accepted beliefs, blending seamlessly into the crowd — while privately holding views that
would get them exiled or killed. They share their real mind only with a trusted few. The freethinker who
survives a dogmatic age by appearing the most devout of all.

**Tells, tactics & dialogue:** Public conformity, private candor. *"Of course I believe as everyone
does"* — said by the one who believes nothing of the sort. A character whose outward respectability hides
a radically different inner life.

**Counter & backfire:** Living a double life is corrosive and dangerous — a slip, a careless word, or a
betrayer who knew the real mind can expose the heretic to the herd's fury. The mask can also swallow the
face: conform long enough and the character may lose the conviction they were hiding. And those tolerant
"friends" who know the secret hold a permanent blade over the character's throat.
