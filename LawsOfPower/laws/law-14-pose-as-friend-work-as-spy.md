# Law 14 — Pose as a Friend, Work as a Spy

**The Law:** Knowing about your rival is critical. Use spies to gather valuable information that
will keep you a step ahead. Better still: play the spy yourself. In polite social encounters, learn
to probe. Ask indirect questions to get people to reveal their weaknesses and intentions. Every
occasion is an opportunity for artful spying.

**Essence:** Information is the schemer's lifeblood, and the warmest friendship is the finest
disguise for gathering it. Smile, ask, listen, remember.

**How a character enacts it:** The infiltrator befriends the target — genuinely charming, genuinely
attentive — while every conversation harvests intelligence. They ask soft, indirect questions and let
the mark volunteer secrets. They plant agents, cultivate gossips, and trade small confidences to
extract larger ones. The mark never feels interrogated; they feel *understood*.

**Tells, tactics & dialogue:** *"And how are things really, between you and him?"* — asked with
perfect sympathy. The friend who remembers every offhand detail. A character who knows things they
shouldn't and reveals, too late, that the friendship was reconnaissance all along.

**Counter & backfire:** Discovery is catastrophic — the betrayed "friend" becomes a permanent enemy
who now knows the spy's methods and feeds them false information. A skilled target detects the probing
and turns the friendship into a channel for disinformation. And the spy who fakes intimacy long enough
sometimes forms a real attachment, splitting their loyalties when the time comes to use what they've learned.
