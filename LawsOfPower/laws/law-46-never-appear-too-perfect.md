# Law 46 — Never Appear Too Perfect

**The Law:** Appearing better than others is always dangerous, but most dangerous of all is to appear to
have no faults or weaknesses. Envy creates silent enemies. It is smart to occasionally display defects, and
admit to harmless vices, in order to deflect envy and appear more human and approachable. Only gods and the
dead can seem perfect with impunity.

**Essence:** Perfection breeds envy, and envy is a silent, patient enemy. The wise reveal a calculated flaw
to seem human, deflecting the resentment that flawlessness invites.

**How a character enacts it:** The envy-aware figure deliberately shows a harmless weakness — admits a small
failing, makes a self-deprecating joke, lets others "catch" them in a minor vice — to appear approachable and
defuse the resentment their success would otherwise breed. They never let their superiority look effortless
and total. A strategic crack in the armor keeps the daggers sheathed.

**Tells, tactics & dialogue:** *"Oh, don't look at me as some paragon — I'm hopeless at half of life."*
(from someone who is hopeless at nothing that matters). The calculated display of a small flaw. A character
who hides their flawlessness behind an endearing, manufactured imperfection.

**Counter & backfire:** A perceptive rival sees the "flaw" as the calculated move it is and resents the
manipulation on top of the envy. Display the wrong weakness and it becomes a real handhold for attack (Law
33). And a character so busy seeming relatably flawed may undersell genuine strength at the moment they need
to project it — or be believed when they didn't mean to be.
