# Law 42 — Strike the Shepherd and the Sheep Will Scatter

**The Law:** Trouble can often be traced to a single strong individual — the stirrer, the arrogant
underling, the poisoner of goodwill. If you allow such people room to operate, others will succumb to
their influence. Do not wait for the troubles they cause to multiply, do not try to negotiate with them —
they are irredeemable. Neutralize their influence by isolating or banishing them. Strike at the source and
the sheep will scatter.

**Essence:** Most group trouble radiates from one charismatic instigator. Remove that single source and
the followers — leaderless and directionless — disperse on their own.

**How a character enacts it:** The strategist identifies the one ringleader behind an opposing faction —
the agitator, the rival's brilliant lieutenant, the keystone of a conspiracy — and focuses everything on
removing or discrediting that single person, rather than fighting the whole crowd. With the shepherd gone,
the flock loses cohesion and scatters. Surgical, not broad.

**Tells, tactics & dialogue:** *"Cut off the head and the body falls."* The character who ignores the
noisy crowd to target the quiet figure orchestrating it. A faction that collapses the moment its leader is
removed.

**Counter & backfire:** Strike the wrong "shepherd" and the real instigator remains while you've made a
martyr of an innocent. Removing a leader can *radicalize* the flock rather than scatter it — grief and
outrage forging a more dangerous movement than before. And a resilient group with distributed leadership
simply replaces the fallen shepherd, leaving the schemer to learn there was never just one head to cut.
