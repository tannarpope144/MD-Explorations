# Law 33 — Discover Each Man's Thumbscrew

**The Law:** Everyone has a weakness, a gap in the castle wall. That weakness is usually an insecurity,
an uncontrollable emotion or need; it can also be a small secret pleasure. Either way, once found, it is
a thumbscrew you can turn to your advantage. Find the chink, the helpless child, the hidden need, and
you have leverage over even the strong.

**Essence:** Every person has one weakness — a fear, a vanity, a craving, a loved one — that, once found,
gives you total leverage. The strongest fortress has a single unguarded gate.

**How a character enacts it:** The manipulator studies a target patiently for the soft spot: the
insecurity they overcompensate for, the vice they hide, the person they'd do anything to protect, the
flattery they can't resist. Then they apply pressure precisely there. Find the thumbscrew and even a
powerful enemy becomes pliable — controlled not by force but by their own need or fear.

**Tells, tactics & dialogue:** *"Everyone has a price. His isn't money — it's his daughter's good
opinion."* The character who watches a rival closely, looking for the crack. Sudden, uncanny influence
over someone who should be untouchable.

**Counter & backfire:** Press the wrong "weakness" and you reveal your hand, arming the target with the
knowledge that you're hunting their vulnerabilities. Some people's apparent weakness is bait (Law 21).
And turning the thumbscrew creates a victim with nothing left to lose — exactly the kind of cornered,
desperate enemy who burns everything down, including the manipulator (Laws 15, 22).
