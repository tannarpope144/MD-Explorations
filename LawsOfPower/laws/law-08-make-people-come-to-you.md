# Law 8 — Make People Come to You

**The Law:** When you force the other person to act, you are the one in control. It is always
better to make your opponent come to you, abandoning their own plans in the process. Lure them
with fabulous gains — then attack. You hold the cards.

**Essence:** Whoever sets the terms of the meeting controls it. Bait the opponent off their
prepared ground and onto yours, where every advantage is yours.

**How a character enacts it:** The patient power-player refuses to chase. They dangle a
temptation — a deal, an apology, a prize — and wait for the rival to come hungry and off-balance,
abandoning their position to do so. They never appear to need anything; the need is always made to
flow the other way. When the opponent arrives on their terms, the trap closes.

**Tells, tactics & dialogue:** *"If he wants it, he knows where to find me."* Studied stillness
while others scramble. The summons disguised as an invitation. A character who makes even powerful
people travel to *them* — and reads weakness in the fact that they came.

**Counter & backfire:** Waiting only works if you genuinely hold something the other wants; a
character who plays aloof with no real leverage is simply ignored. A patient rival can wait *longer*,
turning it into a contest of nerve the baiter loses. And the lured target may smell the trap and come
armed — walking in precisely to spring a counter-ambush.
