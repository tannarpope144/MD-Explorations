# Law 45 — Preach the Need for Change, But Never Reform Too Much at Once

**The Law:** Everyone understands the need for change in the abstract, but on the day-to-day level people
are creatures of habit. Too much innovation is traumatic, and will lead to revolt. If you are new to a
position of power, or an outsider trying to build a power base, make a show of respecting the old way of
doing things. If change is necessary, make it feel like a gentle improvement on the past.

**Essence:** People crave the *idea* of change but rebel against its reality. The wise reformer cloaks
upheaval in the comforting language of tradition and moves slowly enough that no one panics.

**How a character enacts it:** The shrewd new ruler or reformer preaches renewal while making a show of
honoring the old ways — keeping familiar symbols, invoking tradition, framing radical change as a return to
cherished roots. They change substance gradually under a veneer of continuity, so the populace is
transformed before it realizes it. Revolution disguised as restoration.

**Tells, tactics & dialogue:** *"We change nothing — we merely restore what was always meant to be."* The
reformer who keeps the old banners while quietly rewriting everything beneath them. A character who soothes
fear of change with the language of the past.

**Counter & backfire:** Move too fast and the trauma triggers the very revolt the law warns of — the
populace clinging to the old order rises up. But move too slowly and entrenched enemies have time to
organize against the reform. And the disguise can be exposed: a rival who reveals that the "restoration" is
actually radical upheaval can turn the tradition-lovers into a furious mob.
