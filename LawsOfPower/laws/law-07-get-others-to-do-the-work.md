# Law 7 — Get Others to Do the Work, But Take the Credit

**The Law:** Use the wisdom, knowledge, and legwork of other people to further your own cause.
Such assistance not only saves you valuable time and energy, it gives you a godlike aura of
efficiency and speed. In the end your helpers will be forgotten and you will be remembered. Never
do yourself what others can do for you.

**Essence:** Leverage other people's labor and genius, then stand alone in the light of the
result. History remembers the name on the monument, not the hands that cut the stone.

**How a character enacts it:** The credit-taker identifies the brilliant, overlooked subordinate
and quietly harvests their work — presenting it upward as their own, ensuring the true author stays
invisible. They surround themselves with talent and position themselves as the face of everything it
produces. Charm and authority let them claim what others built.

**Tells, tactics & dialogue:** *"I've been developing this idea for some time…"* — said over
someone else's idea. The manager who presents the team's breakthrough in the first person. The
forgotten genius muttering in the wings, *"That was mine."*

**Counter & backfire:** The exploited talent eventually realizes, and a wronged collaborator who
knows the truth is a dangerous enemy (Law 2) — they can expose the fraud and shatter the reputation
(Law 5). Lean on others too completely and you become helpless without them, a hollow figurehead.
The plundered subordinate walking out the door — taking the indispensable knowledge with them — is a
classic reversal.
