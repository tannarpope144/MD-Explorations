# Law 15 — Crush Your Enemy Totally

**The Law:** All great leaders since Moses have known that a feared enemy must be crushed
completely. If one ember is left alight, no matter how dimly it smolders, a fire will eventually
break out. More is lost through stopping halfway than through total annihilation. The enemy will
recover, and will seek revenge.

**Essence:** A wounded enemy is more dangerous than a whole one — they return, embittered, for
revenge. Finish what you start, or don't start it.

**How a character enacts it:** The ruthless victor doesn't gloat over a half-beaten foe — they
eliminate the foe's capacity to ever strike back: strip the titles, the allies, the means, the
hope. Mercy, to this character, is a strategic blunder dressed as virtue. They remember every enemy
they let live who came back to haunt them.

**Tells, tactics & dialogue:** *"A wounded snake is the one that bites."* The total, deliberate
dismantling of an opponent rather than a satisfying-but-partial humiliation. A character chillingly
patient about ensuring there is nothing left to recover from.

**Counter & backfire:** Total destruction can be the very act that defines a character as a monster
and turns neutral parties against them — overwhelming force breeds new enemies out of sympathy.
Pushing an opponent into a corner with no way out (Law 22's surrender denied) makes them fight to the
death. And the crushed enemy's surviving kin, allies, or heir inheriting the vendetta is the engine of
countless revenge plots.
