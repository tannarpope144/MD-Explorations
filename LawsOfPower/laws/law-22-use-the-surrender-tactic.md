# Law 22 — Use the Surrender Tactic: Transform Weakness into Power

**The Law:** When you are weaker, never fight for honor's sake; choose surrender instead. Surrender
gives you time to recover, time to torment and irritate your conqueror, time to wait for their power
to wane. Do not give them the satisfaction of fighting and defeating you — surrender first. By turning
the other cheek you infuriate and unsettle them.

**Essence:** Yielding when outmatched preserves you to fight another day. Surrender is not defeat — it
is a patient, smiling retreat that keeps you alive inside the victor's guard.

**How a character enacts it:** The outmatched survivor bends rather than breaks — submits, flatters the
conqueror, becomes useful and unthreatening — all while waiting for the victor to grow complacent or
weak. From inside the winner's circle they corrode, gather strength, and choose their moment. The
defeated noble who kneels today to undermine the throne from within tomorrow.

**Tells, tactics & dialogue:** *"You've won. I'm yours to command"* — said with a private smile. The
graceful capitulation that's actually a long game. A character who loses every battle on purpose to win
the war.

**Counter & backfire:** A wise victor knows the surrender is a ruse and crushes the kneeling enemy
totally (Law 15) rather than keeping a viper close. Surrender can also become real — bend long enough and
the character may lose the will or the means to ever rise again, the mask of submission becoming their
true face. And allies may read the surrender as cowardice or betrayal and abandon them.
