# Law 19 — Know Who You're Dealing With; Do Not Offend the Wrong Person

**The Law:** There are many different kinds of people in the world, and you can never assume that
everyone will react to your strategies in the same way. Deceive or outmaneuver some people and they
will spend the rest of their lives seeking revenge. Choose your victims and opponents carefully —
never offend or deceive the wrong person.

**Essence:** The same move that crushes one person provokes another into a lifelong vendetta. Read
the target before you act; the cost of misjudging is open-ended.

**How a character enacts it:** The careful operator studies a person before crossing them — their
temperament, their resources, their capacity for grudge. They'll deceive the forgetful and the weak
freely, but tread with elaborate care around the proud, the patient, and the well-connected. The
disaster is the arrogant character who cheats the quiet stranger who turns out to be the most
dangerous person in the kingdom.

**Tells, tactics & dialogue:** *"Do you have any idea who you just insulted?"* The visible
recalculation when a character realizes their mark is not who they assumed. A schemer who probes a
new player's nature before committing to a move against them.

**Counter & backfire:** Even careful reading fails — people hide their true nature (Law 3), and the
meek clerk is sometimes the spymaster. Over-caution means never striking at anyone, frozen by the fear
of picking the wrong target. The whole law is a setup for dramatic irony: the audience often knows
*exactly* who the fool just offended.
