# Law 2 — Never Put Too Much Trust in Friends; Use Your Enemies

**The Law:** Be wary of friends — they envy easily and become tyrannical; they grow
spoiled and undermine you. Hire a former enemy and they will be more loyal than a friend,
because they have more to prove. You have more to fear from friends than from enemies.

**Essence:** Friendship clouds judgment and breeds entitlement; a redeemed enemy, hungry
to justify the mercy shown them, serves with desperate loyalty.

**How a character enacts it:** The cold strategist promotes the rival they just defeated
instead of an old comrade — knowing gratitude and something-to-prove outlast affection.
They keep friends at a careful distance from real power and watch the envy that intimacy
breeds. When betrayed, it's almost always by the friend who felt entitled to more.

**Tells, tactics & dialogue:** *"You spared me. I will not forget it."* The spared enemy's
fervor. A ruler who trusts the converted assassin over the boyhood friend. Friends who say
*"after everything I've done for you…"* — the entitlement that precedes the knife.

**Counter & backfire:** The "loyal" enemy may simply be biding time, loyalty a mask over a
longer grudge. And a character so suspicious of friends that they trust no one becomes
isolated and paranoid (Law 18), surrounded by opportunists with no genuine ally when the
real crisis comes — a tragic, lonely flavor of downfall.
