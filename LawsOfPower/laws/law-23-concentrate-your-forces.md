# Law 23 — Concentrate Your Forces

**The Law:** Conserve your forces and energies by keeping them concentrated at their strongest point.
You gain more by finding a rich mine and mining it deeper than by flitting from one shallow mine to
another. Intensity defeats extensity every time. When looking for sources of power, find the one key
patron, the fat cow who will give you milk for a long time.

**Essence:** Scattered effort achieves nothing; concentrated force at the decisive point breaks
through. Find the single richest source of power and exploit it fully.

**How a character enacts it:** The focused climber attaches themselves to one powerful patron and mines
that relationship for everything rather than spreading thin across many. In conflict, they mass their
strength against the single weak point instead of skirmishing everywhere. They identify the one source —
a benefactor, a stronghold, a secret — that yields the most, and pour everything into it.

**Tells, tactics & dialogue:** *"One patron, well-chosen, is worth a hundred acquaintances."* The
character who ignores a dozen tempting distractions to drive at one objective. Total commitment of
resources to the decisive front.

**Counter & backfire:** Concentrate everything on one patron, one stronghold, one source — and you've
created a single point of failure. When the patron dies, the mine runs dry, or the stronghold falls,
the character loses everything at once. Over-focus also creates blind spots: while massing forces at one
point, they leave their flank open to an enemy who refused to engage where they were strong.
