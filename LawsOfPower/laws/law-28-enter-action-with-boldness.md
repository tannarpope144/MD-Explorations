# Law 28 — Enter Action With Boldness

**The Law:** If you are unsure of a course of action, do not attempt it. Your doubts and hesitations
will infect your execution. Timidity is dangerous: better to enter with boldness. Any mistakes you
commit through audacity are easily corrected with more audacity. Everyone admires the bold; no one
honors the timid.

**Essence:** Hesitation is fatal; boldness commands respect and carries its own momentum. The audacious
move, fully committed, succeeds where the half-measure dies.

**How a character enacts it:** The bold figure commits totally and acts as if success is inevitable —
their certainty becomes contagious, and others fall in line behind it. They make the daring play, seize
the moment without flinching, and when they err, they double down rather than retreat. Audacity itself
intimidates rivals and rallies followers.

**Tells, tactics & dialogue:** *"Hesitate and we lose everything. We move now."* The decisive,
unflinching commitment that sweeps doubters along. A character whose sheer nerve carries a plan that
caution would have killed.

**Counter & backfire:** Boldness uninformed is recklessness — the audacious character who's *wrong*
charges straight off the cliff, and "more audacity" only digs the grave deeper. Their predictability in
always attacking can be turned against them by a patient opponent who lets them overextend. And the bold
move that fails publicly shatters the aura of invincibility, inviting every rival to pounce at once.
