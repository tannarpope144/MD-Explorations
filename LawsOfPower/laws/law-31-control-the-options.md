# Law 31 — Control the Options: Get Others to Play With the Cards You Deal

**The Law:** The best deceptions are the ones that seem to give the other person a choice: your
victims feel they are in control, but are actually your puppets. Give people options that come out in
your favor whichever one they choose. Force them to make choices between the lesser of two evils, both
of which serve your purpose.

**Essence:** True control isn't forcing a choice — it's controlling which choices exist. Frame the
board so every move the victim makes advances your design while they feel perfectly free.

**How a character enacts it:** The puppeteer narrows a target's apparent options down to a few, all of
which serve the schemer. They offer a "free choice" between two paths that both lead where they want,
or make every alternative to the desired one painful. The victim chooses, feels autonomous, and walks
exactly where they were herded.

**Tells, tactics & dialogue:** *"The decision is entirely yours, of course."* (between two doors the
schemer built). The false dilemma. A character who always seems to win no matter what their opponent
decides — because they dealt the cards.

**Counter & backfire:** A perceptive target senses the choices are rigged and refuses to play —
choosing the unscripted option, flipping the table, or simply walking away. Constructing controlled
choices takes elaborate setup that can be disrupted by one unforeseen variable. And being caught rigging
someone's choices breeds a deep, specific betrayal — the victim realizes their every "free" decision
was authored by another.
