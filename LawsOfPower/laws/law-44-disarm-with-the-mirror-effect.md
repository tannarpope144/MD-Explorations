# Law 44 — Disarm and Infuriate with the Mirror Effect

**The Law:** The mirror reflects reality, but it is also the perfect tool for deception. When you mirror
your enemies, doing exactly as they do, they cannot figure out your strategy. The Mirror Effect mocks and
humiliates them, making them overreact. By holding up a mirror to their psyches, you seduce them with the
illusion that you share their values; by holding up a mirror to their actions, you teach them a lesson.

**Essence:** Reflecting someone back at themselves disarms them — they see their own image and lower their
guard, or see their own tactics turned around and lose their composure. The mirror seduces, mocks, or
neutralizes.

**How a character enacts it:** The mirror-user adopts the target's own manner, values, or tactics. To
*seduce*: they reflect a person's tastes and beliefs until the target feels a soulmate has appeared (a
seduction technique). To *humiliate*: they do to an enemy exactly what the enemy does to others, exposing
it. To *neutralize*: they passively echo an opponent's moves so no counter-strategy can grip. The reflection
gives nothing of the mirror-holder away.

**Tells, tactics & dialogue:** *"Funny — that's exactly what you did to me. How does it feel?"* The
uncanny sense of being perfectly understood (seduction) or perfectly thrown back (mockery). A character who
wins by giving an opponent nothing but their own reflection.

**Counter & backfire:** Pure mirroring is reactive — a mirror only ever copies, so a target who recognizes
it can lead the mirror-holder into self-defeating moves. Mocking someone with their own behavior can
enrage them past all reason into a destructive frenzy (Law 39's overflow). And the seductive mirror, held
up long enough, can blur the holder's own identity into the reflection.
