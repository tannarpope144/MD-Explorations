# Law 20 — Do Not Commit to Anyone

**The Law:** It is the fool who always rushes to take sides. Do not commit to any side or cause but
yourself. By maintaining your independence, you become the master of others — playing people against
one another, making them pursue you. Keep yourself free of entanglements and you keep your options
and your leverage.

**Essence:** Commitment to a side surrenders leverage and makes you a tool. Stay uncommitted, and
every faction must court you while you bind yourself to none.

**How a character enacts it:** The independent operator holds back from every alliance, keeping all
sides hopeful and all options open. They let rivals bid for their support, extracting concessions from
each, committing to none until the decisive moment — or never. They are the kingmaker who refuses to
be a subject, valued precisely because they cannot be counted on.

**Tells, tactics & dialogue:** *"I'm a friend to all parties and a servant of none."* The studied
neutrality that keeps everyone guessing and everyone courting. A character who profits from a conflict
by selling something to both sides.

**Counter & backfire:** Commit to no one and eventually no one trusts you — both sides may turn on the
fence-sitter once they realize they'll never have your loyalty. In a war with no neutral ground, the
uncommitted are crushed by whoever wins. And a character who never commits can find, in their final
hour, that they've built no real bonds and have no one willing to stand with them.
