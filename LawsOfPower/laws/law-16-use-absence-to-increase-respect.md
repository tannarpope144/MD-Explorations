# Law 16 — Use Absence to Increase Respect and Honor

**The Law:** Too much circulation makes the price go down: the more you are seen and heard from,
the more common you appear. If you are already established in a group, temporary withdrawal from it
will make you more talked about, even more admired. You must learn when to leave. Create value
through scarcity.

**Essence:** Presence breeds familiarity, and familiarity breeds contempt. Strategic absence makes
the heart grow fonder and the value climb.

**How a character enacts it:** The figure at the height of their charm withdraws — leaves the party
early, vanishes from court for a season, goes quiet in a courtship — and lets longing and rumor inflate
their worth. They ration their presence like a scarce currency. Their return becomes an event. Absence
is wielded as a deliberate move, timed for maximum hunger.

**Tells, tactics & dialogue:** The abrupt, unexplained departure that leaves everyone wanting more.
*"Where did she go? When will he be back?"* A character who understands that being slightly missed beats
being fully available.

**Counter & backfire:** Absence only works once you're valued — withdraw before you matter and you're
simply forgotten, the spot filled by someone present. A rival uses the absence to consolidate power and
poison the well in your name (Law 18). And a lover or ally who feels abandoned rather than tantalized may
not be there when you deign to return.
