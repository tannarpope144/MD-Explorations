# Law 34 — Be Royal in Your Own Fashion: Act Like a King to Be Treated Like One

**The Law:** The way you carry yourself will often determine how you are treated: in the long run,
appearing vulgar or common will make people disrespect you. For a king respects himself and inspires the
same sentiment in others. By acting regally and confident of your powers, you make yourself seem destined
to wear a crown.

**Essence:** The world takes you at the value you set on yourself. Carry yourself like royalty —
demand respect by your bearing — and others will treat you as the figure you project.

**How a character enacts it:** The figure of regal bearing never grovels, never explains, never accepts a
lowly seat. They project total self-certainty, set a high price on their attention, and treat their own
dignity as inviolable — and the crowd, reading the confidence, defers. The pauper who carries themselves
like a prince and is, in time, taken for one.

**Tells, tactics & dialogue:** *"I do not ask. I expect."* Unhurried, unbothered, certain of their own
worth. A character who walks into a room they have no right to and is somehow obeyed because they act as
though obedience is natural.

**Counter & backfire:** Regal bearing with nothing behind it is exposed the moment it's tested — the
pretender who "acts like a king" can be humiliated by someone who calls the bluff (Law 21's mirror).
Cross from confidence into arrogance and the audience longs to see the proud figure fall (Law 46). And a
genuine power who finds the bearing presumptuous may crush the upstart for the insult of pretending to
equality.
