# Law 11 — Learn to Keep People Dependent on You

**The Law:** To maintain your independence you must always be needed and wanted. The more you
are relied on, the more freedom you have. Make people depend on you for their happiness and
prosperity and you have nothing to fear. Never teach them enough that they can do without you.

**Essence:** Indispensability is the truest security. Make yourself the irreplaceable
linchpin — the one whose removal collapses everything — and you cannot be discarded.

**How a character enacts it:** The indispensable schemer ensures critical knowledge, contacts,
or skills run through them alone. They help generously — but never *teach*, never document, never
let anyone learn to do without them. The advisor who makes the king unable to rule without them;
the fixer who's the only one who knows where the bodies are buried.

**Tells, tactics & dialogue:** *"Leave it to me — only I know how this really works."* Helpful on
the surface, hoarding underneath. A character who answers every problem but never trains a
successor. The quiet panic on a powerful person's face when their indispensable servant threatens
to leave.

**Counter & backfire:** A master who realizes they're trapped will move heaven and earth to break
free — and a cornered patron is dangerous (they may destroy the linchpin rather than stay caged).
Manufactured dependence is brittle: the moment someone *else* learns the secret, the irreplaceable
becomes disposable overnight. And it breeds a smothering resentment that fuels betrayal.
