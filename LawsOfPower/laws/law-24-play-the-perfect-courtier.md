# Law 24 — Play the Perfect Courtier

**The Law:** The perfect courtier thrives in a world where everything revolves around power and
political dexterity. They have mastered the art of indirection; they flatter, yield to superiors, and
assert power over others in the most oblique and graceful manner. Learn and apply the laws of
courtiership and there will be no limit to how far you can rise.

**Essence:** In the halls of power, manners are weapons and grace is a strategy. The courtier wins not
by force but by flawless social maneuver — flattery that doesn't cloy, ambition that never shows.

**How a character enacts it:** The consummate courtier flatters with such subtlety it reads as honesty,
makes superiors feel brilliant (Law 1), never shows naked ambition, and attacks rivals through
insinuation rather than open assault. They master the etiquette of the court, blend in (Law 38), and
make their own advancement look like everyone else's good fortune. Every gesture is calculated; none
appears to be.

**Tells, tactics & dialogue:** Honeyed words with a hidden edge. *"You honor us all with your wisdom,
Your Grace"* — meant and unmeant at once. The graceful concession that's actually a maneuver. A
character utterly fluent in the unspoken rules of the room.

**Counter & backfire:** A court is a nest of rival courtiers all playing the same game — the perfect
courtier can be out-flattered, out-maneuvered, or exposed as a sycophant. Master the art too well and
the character becomes all surface, trusted by no one, suspected by all. And a blunt, powerful outsider
who scorns the courtly game can shatter the whole delicate web with one honest blow.
