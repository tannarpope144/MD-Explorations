# Law 35 — Master the Art of Timing

**The Law:** Never seem to be in a hurry — hurrying betrays a lack of control over yourself, and over
time. Always seem patient, as if you know that everything will come to you eventually. Become a detective
of the right moment; sniff out the spirit of the times, the trends that will carry you to power. Learn to
stand back when the time is not yet ripe, and to strike fiercely when it has reached fruition.

**Essence:** The same move triumphs or dies on timing alone. The master waits, reads the moment, and
strikes only when conditions are ripe — patience, then the sudden pounce.

**How a character enacts it:** The patient predator does nothing prematurely. They watch a rival
overextend, an alliance fray, a mood shift — and hold their stroke until the instant it cannot fail. They
manufacture the appearance of calm even while burning inside, because visible impatience signals weakness.
When the moment ripens, they move all at once.

**Tells, tactics & dialogue:** *"Not yet. Wait."* The unnerving calm of someone who knows their hour is
coming. A character who absorbs insults and setbacks without reaction, then strikes devastatingly when
the time is finally right.

**Counter & backfire:** Waiting for the "perfect" moment can mean waiting forever — the over-patient
character lets real opportunities pass while a bolder rival (Law 28) seizes them. Misjudging ripeness
means striking too early or too late, and either is fatal. And a patient schemer can be baited into acting
prematurely by a rival who manufactures a false "perfect moment."
