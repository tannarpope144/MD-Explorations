# Law 9 — Win Through Actions, Never Through Argument

**The Law:** Any momentary triumph you think you have gained through argument is really a
Pyrrhic victory: the resentment and ill will you stir up is stronger and lasts longer than any
momentary change of opinion. Demonstrate, do not explicate. It is much more powerful to get
others to agree with you through your actions.

**Essence:** Words win the argument and lose the person. A deed that demonstrates the truth
convinces without humiliating, and leaves no resentful enemy behind.

**How a character enacts it:** Rather than argue a rival is incompetent, the strategist lets
them fail visibly and steps in to fix it — the deed speaks. Rather than promise loyalty, they
prove it with a costly act. They avoid the tempting public debate, knowing that winning it
creates a humiliated enemy who never forgets the loss.

**Tells, tactics & dialogue:** *"I won't argue with you. Watch."* A character who answers a
challenge with a result instead of a speech. The quiet competence that ends a dispute no
rhetoric could.

**Counter & backfire:** Arranging for a rival's failure can be exposed as sabotage, turning the
demonstration into a scandal. Some situations genuinely demand persuasion *now*, and a character
too proud to argue may lose ground while waiting for events to vindicate them. A rival skilled
with words can frame the "demonstration" as luck or trickery before the lesson lands.
