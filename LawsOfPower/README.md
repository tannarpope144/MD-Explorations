# Rules of Power — Character Simulation Toolkit

A distillation of Robert Greene's *The 48 Laws of Power* into a writer's toolkit for
**simulating the actions, schemes, and decisions of fictional characters.**

Use this to ask: *What would this character do?* When a schemer, courtier, tyrant,
seducer, or survivor faces a situation, these laws model how a power-minded mind reads
the room and moves. The laws are presented **faithfully and unflinchingly** — manipulation,
deception, and ruthlessness included — because that is the raw material of dramatic
character.

> **This is a fiction tool.** The laws describe how scheming, ambitious, and ruthless
> characters *think and act*. They are a lens for writing believable power players,
> antagonists, and morally complex protagonists — not life advice. The most useful field
> in each entry is often **Counter & backfire**: that's where the plot lives.

## How to use it while writing

- **To drive a character's choices:** open the law that fits their instinct and pull from
  *How a character enacts it* and *Tells, tactics & dialogue.*
- **To build a scene's conflict:** two characters running different laws against each
  other *is* the scene. Use *Counter & backfire* to find where a scheme cracks.
- **To cast a trait to a character:** the archetype tags below and in each file help you
  decide who naturally leans on which law.
- **To keep a villain from going cartoonish:** every law has a cost and a way it rebounds.
  Let it.

Load `laws/law-NN-*.md` for the full entry. Each file follows the same shape:
**The Law · Essence · How a character enacts it · Tells, tactics & dialogue · Counter & backfire.**

## Character archetypes (recurring power players)

- **The Schemer / Strategist** — plays long, conceals intent, plans to the end. Laws 3, 14, 20, 29, 31, 33, 35, 39.
- **The Courtier / Operator** — survives and rises inside a court or institution. Laws 1, 19, 24, 26, 38, 46.
- **The Seducer / Charmer** — works on hearts, minds, and fantasies. Laws 12, 13, 27, 32, 43, 44.
- **The Tyrant / Conqueror** — bold, total, intimidating. Laws 15, 17, 22, 28, 34, 37, 42.
- **The Survivor / Chameleon** — adaptable, self-reinventing, never cornered. Laws 10, 18, 25, 36, 45, 48.
- **The Showman / Reputation-builder** — lives by image and attention. Laws 4, 5, 6, 16, 21, 30, 47.
- **The Power-broker** — leverages others, stays indispensable, keeps hands clean. Laws 2, 7, 8, 9, 11, 23, 40, 41.

(Most vivid characters mix archetypes; a single law often suits several.)

## The 48 Laws at a glance

| # | Law | Character instinct in one line |
|---|-----|--------------------------------|
| 1 | Never Outshine the Master | Make the powerful feel superior; hide your own brilliance to survive near them. |
| 2 | Never Put Too Much Trust in Friends; Use Your Enemies | Friends betray from envy; a redeemed enemy serves hardest. |
| 3 | Conceal Your Intentions | Keep them guessing; never reveal the real aim. |
| 4 | Always Say Less Than Necessary | Silence unnerves; the less you say, the more powerful you seem. |
| 5 | Protect Your Reputation at All Costs | Reputation does the fighting for you; defend it ruthlessly. |
| 6 | Court Attention at All Costs | Be seen, be talked about; obscurity is death. |
| 7 | Get Others to Do the Work, Take the Credit | Let others toil; claim the glory. |
| 8 | Make People Come to You | Bait them onto your ground, then strike. |
| 9 | Win Through Actions, Never Argument | Prove with the deed; arguments breed lasting resentment. |
| 10 | Don't Get Infected by Misery and Misfortune | Cut loose the doomed and the unlucky before they drag you down. |
| 11 | Learn to Keep People Dependent on You | Make yourself indispensable so they can never be rid of you. |
| 12 | Use Selective Honesty and Generosity to Disarm | One sincere gift opens the door to deception. |
| 13 | Appeal to Self-Interest, Never to Mercy | Never beg gratitude; show them what they gain. |
| 14 | Pose as a Friend, Work as a Spy | Befriend to extract; smile while you gather their secrets. |
| 15 | Crush Your Enemy Totally | Leave no ember; a wounded enemy returns for revenge. |
| 16 | Raise Your Value Through Absence and Scarcity | Withdraw to be missed; presence breeds contempt. |
| 17 | Keep Others in Suspended Terror (Unpredictability) | Be unreadable; keep everyone off balance and afraid. |
| 18 | Don't Isolate Yourself Behind a Fortress | Isolation blinds and traps; move among the people. |
| 19 | Know Who You're Dealing With | Read your mark before you move; offend the wrong person and pay forever. |
| 20 | Do Not Commit to Anyone | Stay above the fray; let all sides court you. |
| 21 | Play a Sucker to Catch a Sucker | Seem dumber than your mark; let them underestimate you. |
| 22 | Use the Surrender Tactic | Yield to survive; turn weakness into a waiting weapon. |
| 23 | Concentrate Your Forces | Strike at the single richest point; don't scatter your strength. |
| 24 | Play the Perfect Courtier | Master flattery, indirection, and grace inside the halls of power. |
| 25 | Re-Create Yourself | Forge your own myth; refuse the role others assign you. |
| 26 | Keep Your Hands Clean | Use cat's-paws and scapegoats; never be seen at the deed. |
| 27 | Play on People's Need to Believe | Offer a faith, a cause; become the focus of their devotion. |
| 28 | Enter Action With Boldness | Commit utterly; the bold are admired, the timid devoured. |
| 29 | Plan All the Way to the End | Foresee every twist; know your endgame before the first move. |
| 30 | Make Your Accomplishments Seem Effortless | Hide the sweat; let your power seem natural and limitless. |
| 31 | Control the Options | Rig the choices so every path serves you. |
| 32 | Play to People's Fantasies | Sell the dream, never the ugly truth. |
| 33 | Discover Each Man's Thumbscrew | Find the secret weakness; press it to control them. |
| 34 | Be Royal in Your Own Fashion | Carry yourself like a crown and the world bows. |
| 35 | Master the Art of Timing | Strike at the ripe moment; patience, then the pounce. |
| 36 | Disdain Things You Cannot Have | Show contempt for what you can't reach; ignoring is the sharpest revenge. |
| 37 | Create Compelling Spectacles | Dazzle with imagery and symbol; spectacle hides the strings. |
| 38 | Think as You Like, But Behave Like Others | Hide your heresy behind a conventional mask. |
| 39 | Stir Up Waters to Catch Fish | Stay calm; enrage your rivals into self-destruction. |
| 40 | Despise the Free Lunch | Suspect every gift; what's free comes with a hook. |
| 41 | Avoid Stepping Into a Great Man's Shoes | Escape a giant's shadow; carve your own legend. |
| 42 | Strike the Shepherd, Scatter the Sheep | Remove the one source of trouble and the rest disperse. |
| 43 | Work on Hearts and Minds | Seduce belief; coercion only breeds rebellion. |
| 44 | Disarm with the Mirror Effect | Reflect them back to seduce, mock, or vanish your strategy. |
| 45 | Preach Change, But Never Reform Too Much | Promise renewal; change slowly or provoke revolt. |
| 46 | Never Appear Too Perfect | Show a flaw; flawlessness breeds deadly envy. |
| 47 | Do Not Go Past the Mark You Aimed For | Stop at victory; overreach makes new enemies. |
| 48 | Assume Formlessness | Take no fixed shape; be water, impossible to grasp. |
