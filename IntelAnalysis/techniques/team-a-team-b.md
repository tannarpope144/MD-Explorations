# Team A / Team B

**Category:** Emerging analytic technique — contrarian
**Source:** ATP 2-33.4, *Intelligence Analysis* (Appendix A)

## Definition

Team A/Team B is a process for comparing, contrasting, and clarifying two or more equally valid analytic assessments. Multiple teams of analysts each work along different lines of analysis, contrasting two (or more) views or competing hypotheses.

## When to use

When there are at least two competing views within an analytic office, Team A/Team B can clarify the issue. Its value is that it helps opposing groups see merit in each other's perspectives — reducing friction and narrowing differences so everyone can express their views.

> **Note.** If opposing positions are well established, it can be useful to place analysts on teams that advocate positions they normally do not support; forcing analysts to argue the other side tends to make them more aware of their own mindset.

**Caution:** developing a full Team A/Team B requires a significant commitment of analytic time and resources. Consider carefully whether the issue merits this level of attention.

## Method — two phases

### Analytic phase
1. Identify the two or more competing hypotheses.
2. Form teams and designate individuals to develop the best case for each hypothesis.
3. Review information that supports each respective position.
4. Identify missing information that would support or bolster the hypotheses.
5. Prepare structured arguments with explicit discussion of:
   - Key assumptions
   - Key pieces of evidence
   - Articulation of the logic behind the argument

### Debate phase
An oral presentation of the alternative arguments and rebuttals in parallel.
1. Set aside time for a formal debate or an informal brainstorming session.
2. Have an independent jury of peers listen to the presentations and be prepared to question the teams about their assumptions, evidence, and logic.
3. Allow each team to present its case, challenge the other team's arguments, and rebut the opponent's critique.
4. The jury considers the strength of each presentation and recommends possible next steps for further research and collection.
