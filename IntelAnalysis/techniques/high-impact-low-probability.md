# High Impact / Low Probability Analysis

**Category:** Emerging analytic technique — contrarian
**Source:** ATP 2-33.4, *Intelligence Analysis* (Appendix A)

## Definition

High impact/low probability analysis highlights a seemingly unlikely event that would have major consequences if it occurred. It sensitizes analysts to the potential impact of seemingly low-probability events that could have significant repercussions.

## When to use

Mapping out the course of an unlikely yet plausible event can uncover hidden relationships between key factors and assumptions and alert analysts to oversights in the mainstream analytic line. It allows analysts to explore the consequences of an event not deemed likely by conventional wisdom **without** having to challenge the mainline judgment or argue about how likely the event is — a tactful method of communicating a viewpoint that some might prefer not to hear.

Examining the unthinkable lets an analyst develop indicators that may provide early warning of a shift. By periodically reviewing these indicators, the analyst is more likely to counter a prevailing mindset that such a development is highly unlikely.

## Method

1. **Define the high-impact outcome clearly.** This justifies examining what may be deemed a very unlikely development.
2. **Devise one or more plausible pathways** to the low-probability outcome. Be as precise as possible — this aids in developing indicators for later monitoring.
3. **Insert possible triggers or changes in momentum** if appropriate (natural disasters, economic or political shocks).
4. **Brainstorm plausible but unpredictable triggers** of sudden change.
5. **Identify, for each pathway, a set of indicators or observables** that helps anticipate events unfolding in a specific way.
6. **Identify factors that would deflect a bad outcome** or encourage a positive one.

Once the list of indicators is developed, review it periodically.
