# Morphological Analysis with Multiple Scenarios Generation

**Category:** Emerging analytic technique — imaginative
**Source:** ATP 2-33.4, *Intelligence Analysis* (Appendix A)

## Definition

Morphological Analysis is a method for structuring and examining all possible relationships within an unproven, dynamic, and potentially complex environment. Applied with the **Multiple Scenarios Generation** technique, it enables analysts to forecast multiple scenarios — not just worst-case/nightmare scenarios but also those representing favorable conditions for U.S. forces.

## When to use

Best used when:
- There is little to no information available and surprise conditions are likely.
- There is a need to identify variations in threats.
- The analyst needs to understand potential crisis conditions, ascertain driving-force interactions, and comprehend the range of potential scenario outcomes.

**Morphological Analysis:**
- Generates a comprehensive list of potential outcomes and helps identify those more credible or warranting greater attention.
- Focuses analysts and leaders on key actions needed to prepare for future events (right prevention/risk-mitigation orientation).
- Broadens the view of low-probability/high-impact developments, forcing a more objective viewpoint and reducing the chance of dismissing a scenario or key driver at face value.

**Multiple Scenarios Generation:**
- Identifies all possible scenarios and combinations of driving forces for a problem, reducing unforeseen potential.
- Helps identify extreme cases of interaction between an issue's drivers.
- Is similar to [Alternative Futures Analysis](alternative-futures-analysis.md), but the key difference is that it involves **more than one matrix.**
- Uses multiple two-by-two matrices, pairing combinations of multiple key drivers. Each matrix generates four scenarios, so multiple matrices provide multiple potential scenarios.
- Reduces the tendency to miss an outcome — the analyst can revisit the issue via quick screen snapshots and is more likely to notice driver/indicator impacts on events if they unfold unexpectedly.

## Method — two principles

Morphological Analysis operates through **decomposition** and **forced association.**

- **Decomposition:** define a set of key parameters or dimensions, then break each dimension into the relevant forms or states it can assume.
- **Forced association:** every element is paired with and considered in connection with every other element in the morphological space. This narrows possibilities and lets the analyst focus on combinations within the realm of possibility.

Applied using Multiple Scenarios Generation:

1. Define the issue at hand.
2. Identify all key factors, forces, or events influencing the issue (the **drivers**). Define the ends of the spectrum for each driver (less vs. most extreme circumstances) and pair the drivers in two-by-two matrices.
3. Once all driver variations are identified, establish scenarios for each combination (each quadrant in the matrix).
4. Select scenarios that portray a compelling or challenging future not yet considered, and develop indicators to track whether a scenario is developing.

> **Note.** As indicators are developed, analysts may see synergy between certain drivers indicating extreme cases of interaction. This technique is extremely useful in conjunction with [counterfactual reasoning](counterfactual-reasoning.md).
