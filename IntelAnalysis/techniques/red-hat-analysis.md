# Red Hat Analysis

**Category:** Emerging analytic technique — contrarian
**Source:** ATP 2-33.4, *Intelligence Analysis* (Appendix A)

## Definition

Red hat analysis is a reframing technique used to perceive threats and opportunities as others see them, helping analysts avoid **mirror imaging** — the natural tendency to assume that others think and perceive the world the same way they do. It requires the analyst to adopt, and make decisions consistent with, the culture of a foreign leader or group. This technique alone is of limited value without significant cultural understanding of the threat involved.

## When to use

Used to forecast the actions of a threat or competitor. The chances of accuracy are better when foreseeing the behavior of a specific person who has the authority to make decisions; authoritarian leaders and small, cohesive groups (such as terrorist cells) are obvious candidates. Reframing the problem changes the analyst's perspective from that of an observer to that of a leader who must make decisions within the operational culture, usually yielding a more accurate perspective.

## Method

Gather a team of analysts with in-depth knowledge of the operating environment, the target's personality, and the style of thinking. The team should include people who experienced the culture, shared the ethnic background, or worked in a similar environment; including those who understand the target's language is desirable but not strictly necessary. Then:

- Present the team with a situation and ask how they would respond *were they the threat.*
- **Emphasize avoiding mirror imaging.** The question is not "What would you do if you were in the threat's place?" but "How would this person or group in that culture and circumstance most likely think, behave, and respond?"
- In presenting the results, describe the alternatives considered and the rationale for selecting the path the person/group is most likely to take.

Red hat analysis can be organized and conducted by any analyst or team that needs to understand or forecast threat behavior and has (or can gain) access to the required cultural experience. It exploits available resources to develop the best possible analysis of a threat's intent.

## Red Hat vs. Red Team

- **Red Hat analysis** can be conducted by *any* analyst who needs to understand or forecast foreign behavior and can gain access to the required cultural experience. Goal: exploit available resources to develop the best possible analysis of a threat's intent.
- **[Red Team analysis](red-team-analysis.md)** is typically conducted by a *permanent organizational unit or a temporary, specially staffed group* qualified to think like or play the role of a threat. Goal: challenge organizational biases and provide alternative threat COAs.
