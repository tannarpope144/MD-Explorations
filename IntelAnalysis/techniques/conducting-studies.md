# Conducting Studies

**Category:** Diagnostic analytic technique
**Source:** ATP 2-33.4, *Intelligence Analysis*

## Definition

Intelligence analysts complete products such as studies to provide the requesting command or organization with detailed information, assessments, and conclusions about the area of operations (AO) and area of interest. A study can be a systems or functional analysis product and should be as detailed and in-depth as time allows.

## What studies can provide

Studies can support understanding of:

- Local populations.
- Cultures and caste systems.
- Societal systems or organizations.
- Political systems and structures.
- Religions practiced and their impacts.
- Moral beliefs and their impacts.
- Civil authority considerations.
- Military organizations, structures, and equipment.
- Attitudes toward U.S., multinational, or host-nation forces.

Studies can also include the views and attitudes of multinational and host-nation forces toward these factors.

## Method — seven sequential steps

1. Verify the details of the task assigned.
2. Request clarification of the task if needed.
3. Identify existing information and intelligence relevant to the task.
4. Compile existing information into the determined format.
5. Identify gaps in information and intelligence that need to be researched.
6. Begin researching material.
7. Combine the sets of information into a complete study.

## Two study tasks

A complete study includes two tasks:

**Conduct area, regional, or country study.**
Study and provide mission-focused knowledge of the terrain and weather, civil considerations, and threat characteristics for a specified area or region of a foreign country, including the attitudes of the populace and leaders toward joint, multinational, or host-nation forces. Human terrain teams — part of the Army human terrain system — can support area and regional studies; they develop, train, and integrate social science research and analysis to support operationally relevant decision-making.

**Conduct specified study.**
Provide focused knowledge of the terrain and weather, civil considerations, and threat characteristics for a specified topic or requirement. Studies provide the requesting command or organization with detailed information, assessments, and conclusions on the area of interest.
