# Applying Theory

**Category:** Emerging analytic technique — structured
**Source:** ATP 2-33.4, *Intelligence Analysis* (Appendix A)

## Definition

Applying theory begins with formulating a theory based on the evaluation of other examples of the same phenomenon. It is based on the assumption that when a given set of conditions arises, certain other conditions will follow.

## When to use

Use to analyze threat, neutral, and friendly entity patterns of behavior when a large quantity of data is available on an entity over time. It is effective when predicting how large constructs — nations, religions, or ethnic groups — may act or react. (Example: if an insurgency begins to develop in Turkey, analysts can apply what they know about other countries that faced similar problems to predict how Turkey will react militarily and politically.)

## Advantage

One advantage in intelligence analysis is that it **economizes thought.** By identifying the key elements of a problem, applying theory enables an analyst to sort through a mass of less significant details, see beyond transient developments, recognize which trends are superficial, and foresee future developments for which there may be little concrete evidence at the moment.

## Related

- Contrast with [comparison](comparison.md): applying theory is generated from examining a **large** number of cases, whereas comparison draws conclusions from a **small** number of cases.
