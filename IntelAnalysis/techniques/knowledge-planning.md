# Knowledge Planning

**Category:** Emerging analytic technique — structured
**Source:** ATP 2-33.4, *Intelligence Analysis* (Appendix A)

## Definition

Knowledge planning is a dynamic and collaborative methodology for identifying and satisfying critical information needs.

## When to use

Knowledge planning provides a mechanism for linking operational requirements with information collection strategies and capabilities. It links critical information needs with:

- Relevant knowledge centers (Defense Intelligence Enterprise, Intelligence Warfighting Function).
- Collection assets.
- Specific target areas.
- Operational parameters (time, accuracy, resolution).
- Standing collection requirements.

(Example: developing a knowledge plan for a brigade combat team scheduled to deploy to a foreign country.)

## Method — nine steps

Each step is owned by the decision-maker/operations or by intelligence, as noted:

1. **Review and refine critical information needs** (decision-maker and operations).
2. **Determine priorities** (decision-maker and operations).
3. **Determine target areas** (decision-maker and operations).
4. **Determine critical information needs priority and time, accuracy, resolution parameters** (decision-maker and operations).
5. **Review and refine information and intelligence requirements** (intelligence).
6. **Determine requirements** (decision-maker and operations).
7. **Determine sensor requirements** (intelligence).
8. **Determine knowledge center requirements** (intelligence).
9. **Determine processing and dissemination requirements** (intelligence).
