# Technique Selector

**Category:** Reference — choosing a technique (Chapter 6 quick-reference tables)
**Source:** ATP 2-33.4, *Intelligence Analysis*

A quick-reference guide to which analytic techniques fit which purpose and operation type. These tables are a **guide, not a limit** on an analyst's creativity. The most significant factor in choosing a technique during decisive action is **time** (both the time available to analyze and how timely the assessment must be for the decision-maker) — and whether the intelligence element is equipped to apply the technique.

**Operation-type columns:**
- **O/D** = Offensive / Defensive operations
- **Stab** = Stability operations
- **DSCA** = Defense Support of Civil Authorities

An **X** marks operations in which the technique is most frequently used (absence does not forbid use — it indicates it is less typically employed there).

## Quick "what do I use?" notes

- **Diagnostic techniques** (deception detection, key assumptions check, quality of information check, indicators) are used with *most* other techniques to strengthen assessments — treat them as cross-cutting, not standalone.
- **Stability and DSCA** operations make the widest use of the techniques; offensive/defensive operations use a focused subset (note the many O/D blanks above).
- For **deception concerns**, pair [Deception Detection](../techniques/deception-detection.md) with [ACH](../techniques/analysis-of-competing-hypotheses.md) (treat deception as one hypothesis).
- For **avoiding mirror imaging**, use [Red Hat](../techniques/red-hat-analysis.md) (any analyst) or [Red Team](../techniques/red-team-analysis.md) (dedicated unit).
- For **unknown-size/uncertain futures**, escalate from [Alternative Futures](../techniques/alternative-futures-analysis.md) to [Morphological Analysis](../techniques/morphological-analysis.md) (multiple matrices).
