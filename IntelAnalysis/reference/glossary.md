## Terms

**All-Source Intelligence** *(Army)* — The integration of intelligence and information from all relevant sources to analyze situations or conditions that impact operations. (ADRP 2-0)

**Defensive Task** — A task conducted to defeat an enemy attack, gain time, economize force, and develop conditions favorable for offensive or stability tasks. (ADRP 3-0)

**Indications** *(Joint)* — In intelligence usage, information in various degrees of evaluation, all of which bear on the intention of a potential enemy to adopt or reject a course of action. (JP 2-0)

**Indicator** *(Joint)* — An item of information that reflects the intention or capability of an adversary to adopt or reject a course of action. (JP 2-0) *(See [Indicators](../techniques/indicators.md).)*

**Offensive Task** — A task conducted to defeat and destroy enemy forces and seize terrain, resources, and population centers. (ADRP 3-0)

**Site Exploitation** *(Joint)* — A series of activities to recognize, collect, process, preserve, and analyze information, personnel, and/or materiel found during the conduct of operations. (JP 3-31)

**Stability Operations** *(Joint)* — An overarching term encompassing various military missions, tasks, and activities conducted outside the United States in coordination with other instruments of national power to maintain or reestablish a safe and secure environment, provide essential governmental services, emergency infrastructure reconstruction, and humanitarian relief. (JP 3-0)

## Core analysis terms (from Chapter 1)

- **Analysis** — the examination of information in detail to understand it better and evaluate data to develop knowledge or conclusions.
- **Information** — processed data of every description that may be used when conducting analysis; generally answers who, what, where, and when.
- **Knowledge** — the fact or condition of knowing something with familiarity gained through experience or association, derived from information, facts, and descriptions.
- **The intelligence analysis process** — the three steps applied in both single-source and all-source analysis:
  - **Evaluate** — assign a value to the data based on the source and application of the data.
  - **Analyze** — dissect data from information reports into each subcomponent.
  - **Synthesize** — combine the data with previous holdings to update products, create new assessments, or corroborate existing ones (new data may change previously accepted assessments).

## The 12 analytic standards (ICD 203)

Per Intelligence Community Directive 203, intelligence analysis should be: **objective**; **timely**; **based on all available sources of intelligence**; exhibit proper standards of **analytic tradecraft**; properly **describe the quality and reliability of underlying sources**; properly **caveat and express uncertainties or confidence** in judgments; properly **distinguish underlying factual intelligence from the assumptions and judgments** used to form a conclusion; **consider and explain alternative hypotheses**; be **relevant** to the consumer (providing context, warning, or opportunity analysis); **facilitate clear understanding** of the information and reasoning (logical argumentation); be **consistent** with previous production (or highlight and explain any change); and be **accurate.**
