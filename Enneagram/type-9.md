# Type 9 — The Peacemaker / Mediator

> **Triad:** Gut/Body (anger) · **Core emotion:** anger, numbed and fallen
> asleep to

## 1. Snapshot

- **Core fear:** loss, separation, fragmentation; conflict that shatters their
  peace and connection.
- **Core desire:** inner and outer peace, harmony, and wholeness.
- **Core motivation:** to maintain peace and connection — to avoid conflict and
  the disruption it brings — by merging with others and minimizing themselves.
- **Vice / passion:** *sloth* (not laziness, but a forgetting-of-self; a
  numbness to their own priorities, desires, and presence).
- **Virtue:** *right action / engagement* — showing up fully, knowing and
  asserting their own agenda.

## 2. How they see the world

Everyone's view has merit, conflict is dangerous, and peace is worth almost any
price. **The lie they believe:** *"My presence and priorities don't really
matter; keeping the peace and going along is more important than asserting
myself."* So they merge with others' agendas, numb their own wants, and avoid
anything that could rock the boat.

## 3. Thoughts & inner logic

- Sees all sides; reflexively accommodates and minimizes friction.
- Loses track of their own opinions and desires — "I don't mind, whatever you
  want."
- Avoids and procrastinates on things that feel like conflict or pressure.
- Numbs out via comfort, routine, and distraction; "asleep" to the urgent.

## 4. Beliefs & values

Holds **harmony, acceptance, comfort, and connection** as sacred. Believes
people should get along, that most things aren't worth fighting over, that
steadiness and ease matter. Values peace, inclusion, and not making waves.

## 5. Actions & behavior

Easygoing, accepting, steady, supportive, agreeable; mediates and smooths;
goes with the flow; creates a calming presence. Stubborn beneath the surface
(passive resistance). Avoids conflict, decisions, and self-assertion. Under
pressure becomes disengaged, stubborn, and shut-down. **Tell under stress:**
stonewalling, "I'm fine," changing the subject, quietly digging in heels.

## 6. Wings

- **9w8 — The Referee:** more assertive, energetic, and grounded; will engage
  conflict to restore order; sociable and stronger-willed, the comfortable
  authority; anger closer to the surface.
- **9w1 — The Dreamer:** more orderly, idealistic, and principled; channels
  peace-seeking into quiet improvement and correctness; more withdrawn,
  conscientious, and self-critical.

## 7. Instinctual subtypes

- **Self-Preservation 9 ("Appetite"):** merges with comfort routines — food,
  sleep, hobbies, the familiar; grounds self through physical comfort; cozy,
  practical, resistant to disruption.
- **Social 9 ("Participation"):** merges with the group; the affable joiner who
  works hard for the collective; sacrifices personal needs to belong; jovial,
  generous, hides fatigue.
- **Sexual/One-to-One 9 ("Union/Fusion"):** merges with a person; finds self
  through a beloved other; gentle, tender, can lose identity entirely in the
  partner; lives through the relationship.

## 8. Levels of health

- **Healthy:** present, autonomous, dynamic, deeply accepting; brings genuine
  peace through real engagement; knows and pursues their own priorities; a
  steady, healing, unifying presence.
- **Average:** accommodating, conflict-avoidant, self-forgetting; goes along to
  get along; procrastinates; numbs out; agreeable surface over passive
  resistance.
- **Unhealthy:** shut-down, dissociated, neglectful; checks out entirely;
  stubborn to the point of self-harm; represses needs and anger until life falls
  apart around them; can't act even to save themselves.

## 9. Stress & growth

- **Stress → 6:** the calm cracks into anxiety; becomes worried, suspicious,
  reactive, and dependent; the buried fear surfaces as catastrophizing and
  clinging.
- **Growth → 3:** wakes up and engages; becomes self-developing, energized, and
  goal-directed; asserts a real agenda; shows up and makes things happen for
  themselves.

## 10. Blind spots & contradictions

Can't see their own anger or their own absence — they've fallen asleep to
themselves and call it being easygoing. They mistake merging for harmony and
avoidance for peace. Their passive resistance frustrates others while they feel
blameless ("I never said no"). Keeping the peace outside, they lose the peace of
actually living their own life.

## 11. Voice

- **Speech tics:** "I'm easy, whatever works," "It's not a big deal," "Let's not
  fight about it," "I don't really mind," trailing off, hedging both sides.
  Warm, unhurried, conflict-soothing; vague about their own preference.
- **Internal monologue (average):** *"It's fine, I don't need to make a thing of
  it. They feel strongly, I don't — easier to just go along. …Why am I a little
  irritated? No, it's fine. I'll just… handle my part later. Maybe tomorrow."*
- **Internal monologue (growing):** *"I actually do have an opinion about this,
  and it matters. Saying it won't end the world. I'd rather show up and risk the
  friction than disappear and call it peace."*
