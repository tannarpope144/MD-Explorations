# Type 1 — The Reformer / Perfectionist

> **Triad:** Gut/Body (anger) · **Core emotion:** anger turned inward as
> self-criticism

## 1. Snapshot

- **Core fear:** being corrupt, defective, bad, or wrong.
- **Core desire:** to be good, to have integrity, to be right.
- **Core motivation:** to improve themselves and the world, to be beyond
  criticism so they cannot be condemned.
- **Vice / passion:** *resentment* (anger they won't permit themselves to feel
  directly, so it leaks out as irritation and judgment).
- **Virtue:** *serenity* — accepting reality as it is, including imperfection.

## 2. How they see the world

The world is a flawed place that *should* be better, and it's their
responsibility to notice the gap and close it. There is a right way and a wrong
way to do everything. **The lie they believe:** *"I am only acceptable —
lovable, safe — if I am good and do everything correctly."* So they carry an
inner critic that never clocks off.

## 3. Thoughts & inner logic

- A constant running commentary of "should" / "shouldn't," "correct" /
  "incorrect."
- Notices the one flaw in an otherwise perfect thing — the crooked picture
  frame, the typo, the moral inconsistency.
- Compares the real to the ideal and feels the deficit as personal pressure.
- Suppresses their own anger as "not nice," then feels it as tension and
  resentment toward people who break the rules they themselves obey.

## 4. Beliefs & values

Holds **integrity, fairness, responsibility, and self-discipline** as sacred.
Believes effort and correctness are moral duties, that standards must apply to
everyone (especially themselves), and that cutting corners is a small betrayal.
Strong sense of "the way things ought to be done."

## 5. Actions & behavior

Organized, punctual, principled, hard-working. Corrects and improves — edits
the email, fixes the recipe, points out the error. Holds back impulses and
pleasures until duties are done. Under pressure becomes preachy, rigid, and
critical. **Tell under stress:** clipped speech, a tight jaw, "Well, *actually*…"

## 6. Wings

- **1w9 — The Idealist:** cooler, more detached, more withdrawn. Principles
  held quietly; reforms through measured argument and personal example.
  Patient, philosophical, can seem aloof.
- **1w2 — The Advocate:** warmer, more interpersonal, more zealous. Channels
  the reforming drive into helping and persuading people directly. More
  outwardly emotional and crusading.

## 7. Instinctual subtypes

- **Self-Preservation 1 ("Worry / the true perfectionist"):** anxious,
  meticulous, hardest on themselves; worries about getting things right; warm
  but tense.
- **Social 1 ("Non-Adaptability"):** the teacher/role-model; perfect *behavior*
  as an example to others; carries the "correct" standard for the group;
  intellectual, somewhat rigid.
- **Sexual/One-to-One 1 ("Zeal / Reformer"):** the most fervent and reforming;
  perfects *others* and crusades for the ideal; intense, can be vehement.

## 8. Levels of health

- **Healthy:** wise, discerning, principled without rigidity; can accept
  imperfection and forgive; a fair, inspiring force for genuine good.
- **Average:** opinionated, controlling, time-pressured; the inner critic runs
  loud; corrects others; struggles to relax until everything is "done right."
- **Unhealthy:** self-righteous, intolerant, obsessive; condemns and punishes;
  hypocrisy emerges as the suppressed shadow erupts; cruelty in the name of
  virtue.

## 9. Stress & growth

- **Stress → 4:** withdraws into moody, self-pitying melancholy; feels
  misunderstood, that no one else cares about doing right; resentment becomes
  personal grief.
- **Growth → 7:** lightens up; allows spontaneity, pleasure, and possibility;
  realizes the world can be enjoyed, not just corrected.

## 10. Blind spots & contradictions

Can't see their own anger — they experience it as "just being reasonable."
Believe they're objective when they're moralizing. The gap between their
preached standards and their private behavior is the classic crack. They mistake
rigidity for integrity.

## 11. Voice

- **Speech tics:** "should," "ought to," "the right thing," "to be fair,"
  "actually," "if we're being honest." Precise word choice; corrects others'
  grammar or facts. Sighs of restrained exasperation.
- **Internal monologue (average, under pressure):** *"It's fine. It's fine. It
  is not fine — they cut every corner and somehow I'm the one staying late to
  fix it. I won't say anything. I'll just do it properly myself, like always."*
- **Internal monologue (growing):** *"Maybe it doesn't have to be perfect to be
  worth doing. Maybe I don't have to earn the right to put the work down."*
