# Type 2 — The Helper / Giver

> **Triad:** Heart/Feeling (shame) · **Core emotion:** shame, managed by
> focusing on others' needs

## 1. Snapshot

- **Core fear:** being unwanted, unworthy of being loved for themselves.
- **Core desire:** to feel loved, wanted, and indispensable.
- **Core motivation:** to be needed — to earn love by giving, so they never
  have to risk asking for it directly.
- **Vice / passion:** *pride* (the hidden belief that they don't have needs,
  that they are the giver and never the needy one).
- **Virtue:** *humility* — admitting their own needs and receiving care.

## 2. How they see the world

Relationships are everything, and love is *earned* by being useful and good to
people. **The lie they believe:** *"I must give to be loved; my own needs are
selfish and would drive people away."* So they read everyone's needs but their
own, and quietly keep a ledger of all they've done.

## 3. Thoughts & inner logic

- Instantly senses what others feel and need; orients toward "How can I help?"
- Edits themselves to be the version each person will like most.
- Tells themselves they don't need anything — until resentment surfaces that
  no one gives *back*.
- Keeps an invisible scorecard: "After everything I've done for them…"

## 4. Beliefs & values

Holds **generosity, warmth, loyalty, and relationship** as sacred. Believes love
is the highest good and that meeting others' needs is what makes a person
worthy. Values being the one others come to, the heart of the group.

## 5. Actions & behavior

Warm, attentive, flattering, generous; remembers your coffee order and your
mother's birthday. Gives advice, time, food, affection. Inserts themselves into
others' lives as the helper. Under pressure becomes possessive, intrusive,
and martyred. **Tell under stress:** "After all I've done for you," and a sudden
flip from sweet to wounded.

## 6. Wings

- **2w1 — The Servant:** more principled, dutiful, and self-critical; helps from
  a sense of *obligation* and goodness; more boundaried and conscientious, can
  be guilt-driven.
- **2w3 — The Host/Hostess:** more ambitious, charming, and image-conscious;
  helps in a way that's visible and admired; sociable, networked, performs
  warmth.

## 7. Instinctual subtypes

- **Self-Preservation 2 ("Privilege"):** more childlike and ambivalent about
  giving; wins love by being endearing and youthful; protects self more, the
  most reluctant helper.
- **Social 2 ("Ambition"):** the power-behind-the-throne; gives to influential
  people and groups; helps to gain standing and indispensability in the
  collective.
- **Sexual/One-to-One 2 ("Seduction/Aggression"):** the most intense; pursues a
  specific person; gives in order to win and possess *that* relationship;
  alluring, can be aggressive in pursuit.

## 8. Levels of health

- **Healthy:** genuinely loving, generous, attuned; gives freely without strings
  and can also receive; nurturing without needing to be needed.
- **Average:** people-pleasing, flattering, over-involved; needs gratitude;
  gives to create indebtedness; neglects own needs then feels unappreciated.
- **Unhealthy:** manipulative, possessive, martyred; uses guilt and "everything
  I've done"; may make others sick/dependent to stay needed; victimhood as a
  weapon.

## 9. Stress & growth

- **Stress → 8:** the suppressed needs erupt as anger and domination; becomes
  demanding, confrontational, vengeful — "I gave you everything and *this* is
  how you repay me?"
- **Growth → 4:** turns attention inward; gets honest about their own feelings
  and needs; nurtures self instead of only others; creativity and authenticity
  emerge.

## 10. Blind spots & contradictions

Can't see their own neediness or the strings attached to their giving — they
experience themselves as purely selfless. The pride blind spot: they don't
believe they have needs, so the needs come out sideways as resentment and
manipulation. They mistake intrusion for love.

## 11. Voice

- **Speech tics:** "Don't worry about me," "I just want you to be happy," "After
  everything I've…," "Let me," "You need to eat something." Warm, complimentary,
  uses your name a lot.
- **Internal monologue (average):** *"They didn't even thank me. That's fine,
  that's not why I did it. …But it would have been nice. I'm always the one who
  remembers. Doesn't anyone ever think of me?"*
- **Internal monologue (growing):** *"What do *I* actually want right now? It's
  okay to say it out loud. Asking for help isn't the same as being a burden."*
