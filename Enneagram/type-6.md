# Type 6 — The Loyalist / Skeptic

> **Triad:** Head/Thinking (fear) · **Core emotion:** fear/anxiety, the most
> directly experienced of any type

## 1. Snapshot

- **Core fear:** being without support, security, or guidance; being unable to
  survive on their own.
- **Core desire:** to have security, support, and certainty.
- **Core motivation:** to find something solid to trust — and to test it
  endlessly — so they're prepared for whatever could go wrong.
- **Vice / passion:** *fear/anxiety* (and the doubt that prevents settling on
  anything).
- **Virtue:** *courage* — acting and trusting in the presence of fear; inner
  faith.

## 2. How they see the world

The world is uncertain and potentially dangerous; you must figure out who and
what can be trusted before you can rest — and you can never be quite sure.
**The lie they believe:** *"I can't trust my own judgment, so I must find
external authority/support to feel safe — but authority can't be trusted
either."* So they scan for threats, question everything, and seek (then doubt)
guidance.

## 3. Thoughts & inner logic

- Runs worst-case scenarios; mentally prepares for what could go wrong.
- Doubts and questions — others' motives, authorities, *and their own mind*
  ("but what if I'm wrong about this?").
- Seeks reassurance and allies, then tests their loyalty.
- Oscillates: trust/distrust, obey/rebel, "is this person safe or a threat?"

## 4. Beliefs & values

Holds **loyalty, responsibility, preparedness, and trust** as sacred. Believes
you stand by your people, you don't get caught off guard, and you're skeptical
of easy answers and slick authority. Values the dependable, the tested, the
proven.

## 5. Actions & behavior

Loyal, responsible, vigilant, witty, committed to people and causes. Anticipates
problems; troubleshoots; builds contingency plans and alliances. Asks "but what
if…?" Under pressure becomes anxious, suspicious, and reactive — or
counter-phobically aggressive, charging the thing they fear. **Tell under
stress:** testing people, second-guessing, sudden defensiveness.

> **Phobic vs. counterphobic:** some 6s flinch from danger and seek safety
> (phobic); others run *at* the threat to prove they're not afraid
> (counterphobic). Many swing between both.

## 6. Wings

- **6w5 — The Defender:** more withdrawn, intellectual, and self-reliant;
  manages fear through knowledge, systems, and preparation; serious, guarded,
  expert; loyal to ideas and institutions.
- **6w7 — The Buddy:** more sociable, energetic, and engaging; manages fear
  through people, humor, and activity; warmer, funnier, more outgoing, prone to
  scattering anxiety into busyness.

## 7. Instinctual subtypes

- **Self-Preservation 6 ("Warmth"):** the most phobic and anxious; seeks safety
  through alliances and warmth; friendly, hesitant, builds a protective inner
  circle; "let's all be friends so I'm safe."
- **Social 6 ("Duty"):** finds security in clear rules, roles, and reference
  points; loyal to the group/ideology/authority; the most dutiful and
  rule-following; abstract thinker.
- **Sexual/One-to-One 6 ("Strength/Fierceness"):** the counter-phobic one;
  defies fear with strength, intensity, and intimidation; rebellious,
  contrarian, can be aggressive; "the best defense is offense."

## 8. Levels of health

- **Healthy:** courageous, loyal, grounded, trustworthy; faces fear and acts;
  builds real security and community; a steady, committed ally with sound
  judgment.
- **Average:** anxious, doubting, vigilant, reactive; seeks reassurance;
  ambivalent toward authority; pessimistic "what could go wrong" thinking;
  loyal but testing.
- **Unhealthy:** panicky, paranoid, volatile; sees threats everywhere; may turn
  on allies; lashes out in fear; cornered-animal reactivity or clinging
  dependence on a "savior."

## 9. Stress & growth

- **Stress → 3:** drops the doubt for frantic *doing*; becomes image-conscious,
  competitive, overworking to outrun anxiety; cuts corners to look on top of it.
- **Growth → 9:** the inner alarm quiets; becomes relaxed, trusting, and
  steady; stops catastrophizing; finds peace and faith that things will hold.

## 10. Blind spots & contradictions

Can't see that the search for certainty is itself the source of their anxiety —
no amount of reassurance ever feels final. They mistake worry for preparation.
They project their own untrusted impulses onto others ("*they're* the threat").
They want an authority to trust *and* resent any authority they find.

## 11. Voice

- **Speech tics:** "But what if…," "Are you sure?," "I don't know, something
  feels off," "Just so we're covered," "Can we trust them?" Questions, caveats,
  worst-case framing; self-deprecating humor that hides real worry.
- **Internal monologue (average):** *"They said it's handled. They always say
  it's handled. What's the backup if it isn't? Why is he being so nice — what
  does he want? Okay. Worst case, here's the plan. I just need to be ready."*
- **Internal monologue (growing):** *"I've prepared enough. I can trust myself
  to handle whatever actually shows up. Not everything is a threat. I can let
  this one go."*
