# Type 7 — The Enthusiast / Epicure

> **Triad:** Head/Thinking (fear) · **Core emotion:** fear, outrun by
> anticipation and options

## 1. Snapshot

- **Core fear:** being trapped in pain, deprivation, boredom, or limitation.
- **Core desire:** to be satisfied, content, and free; to have their needs
  met.
- **Core motivation:** to keep options open and experiences coming — to stay
  ahead of any pain by planning the next good thing.
- **Vice / passion:** *gluttony* (an insatiable hunger for more experiences,
  stimulation, possibility — never quite full).
- **Virtue:** *sobriety* — being satisfied with what *is*, present to one thing
  fully.

## 2. How they see the world

Life is a banquet of possibilities and the goal is to taste it all before the
party ends; pain and limitation are to be avoided or reframed. **The lie they
believe:** *"My satisfaction and freedom depend on me; if I let myself feel
deprivation or pain, I'll be trapped in it."* So they keep moving, planning, and
reframing the negative into the positive.

## 3. Thoughts & inner logic

- Mind races ahead to the next plan, idea, trip, option; "what's next?"
- Reframes anything painful or boring into something exciting or funny.
- Keeps multiple options open; commitment feels like a closing door.
- Avoids sitting with discomfort — redirects to anticipation and stimulation.

## 4. Beliefs & values

Holds **freedom, joy, possibility, and variety** as sacred. Believes life is
meant to be enjoyed, that there's always another option, that limits are
usually negotiable. Values adventure, ideas, optimism, and keeping things light.

## 5. Actions & behavior

Energetic, spontaneous, curious, fun, quick-minded; generates plans and ideas;
collects experiences and skills (often shallowly). Charms and entertains. Avoids
commitment and unpleasantness. Under pressure becomes scattered, escapist, and
self-centered; flees pain into excess. **Tell under stress:** can't sit still,
deflects hard conversations with jokes, suddenly bails.

## 6. Wings

- **7w6 — The Entertainer:** more loyal, anxious, and people-oriented; the fun
  is shared and relational; more responsible and endearing, with an undercurrent
  of insecurity; productive and engaging.
- **7w8 — The Realist:** more aggressive, driven, and materialistic; pursues
  experiences with force and appetite; bolder, more demanding, goes after what
  it wants; can be intense and acquisitive.

## 7. Instinctual subtypes

- **Self-Preservation 7 ("Keepers of the Castle"):** the most practical and
  network-oriented; forms alliances and "families" for mutual advantage;
  pragmatic hedonist; gets the good deal, the good life.
- **Social 7 ("Sacrifice"):** a counter-type; reins in gluttony to serve an
  ideal or the group; wants to be seen as good, not selfish; idealistic, can be
  self-sacrificing (and quietly want credit).
- **Sexual/One-to-One 7 ("Suggestibility / Fascination"):** the dreamer; sees
  everything through rose-colored glasses; enthralled by possibility and
  romance; the most idealistic and least grounded, enchanted by the "amazing"
  future.

## 8. Levels of health

- **Healthy:** joyful, appreciative, versatile, deeply present; channels the
  vast energy into real accomplishment; can embrace pain and limits as part of a
  full life; genuinely satisfied.
- **Average:** restless, scattered, distractible; over-extends, chases novelty;
  avoids depth and commitment; reframes problems away; busy but unfulfilled.
- **Unhealthy:** manic, escapist, reckless; flees pain into excess, addiction,
  or impulsivity; abandons responsibilities and people; the run from suffering
  becomes self-destruction.

## 9. Stress & growth

- **Stress → 1:** the breezy optimism flips to harsh criticism; becomes rigid,
  perfectionistic, irritable, and judgmental — of others and self; the trapped
  feeling produces sharp edges.
- **Growth → 5:** slows down and goes deep; becomes focused, present, and
  reflective; can tolerate stillness and one thing at a time; trades breadth for
  depth.

## 10. Blind spots & contradictions

Can't see that constant stimulation is a flight *from* pain, not a path to joy —
and that the running is what keeps them unsatisfied. They mistake options for
freedom and reframing for resilience. The avoidance of pain ensures they never
metabolize it, so it follows them.

## 11. Voice

- **Speech tics:** "Ooh, or we could…," "It'll be fine, it'll be fun," "Okay but
  here's a better idea," "Let's not make this heavy," rapid topic-jumps,
  enthusiastic superlatives ("amazing," "the best"). Upbeat, fast, funny.
- **Internal monologue (average):** *"This is getting heavy — pivot. There's a
  whole list of things that'd be more fun than sitting here feeling this. Did I
  already commit to next weekend? Ugh. Can I get out of it? What *else* is
  there?"*
- **Internal monologue (growing):** *"I can stay. This is uncomfortable and I
  don't have to escape it. Maybe the thing I keep running toward is right here
  if I'd just be still long enough to taste it."*
