# P161 — Functions of Money

**Category:** National Economy
**Tags:** money, medium-of-exchange, store-of-value, unit-of-account
**Source:** Basic Economics (Sowell), Ch. 13 — The National Economy

## Principle
Money serves three distinct functions: (1) medium of exchange — it eliminates the
need for a double coincidence of wants that barter requires; (2) store of value —
it allows purchasing power to be held across time; (3) unit of account — it provides
a common measure that makes prices and economic calculation possible. When money
fails in any of these functions — through hyperinflation, for example — economic
coordination becomes drastically harder.

## In plain English
Money is not just coins and bills — it is the technology that makes large-scale
economic specialization possible. Without a common medium of exchange, you are
limited to what you can barter directly; without a store of value, you can't
save or plan; without a unit of account, you can't compare prices.

## The fallacy it corrects
"Money is just paper and has no intrinsic value; it is arbitrary." Money has value
because it performs functions that enable vastly more productive cooperation than
barter. Its value is not intrinsic to the paper but derives from the functions it
reliably performs. When a currency fails to perform those functions (hyperinflation),
people abandon it and find substitutes.

## How it played out
- **Sowell's cases:** Sowell traces how the introduction of money in historical
  economies multiplied the scope of specialization and trade possible — replacing
  the severe constraints of barter with a flexible medium that enabled much deeper
  division of labor.
- **Broader cases:** Zimbabwe's hyperinflation (2007–09) destroyed the store-of-value
  function: workers demanded payment multiple times a day as currency became worthless
  within hours. Zimbabweans switched to foreign currencies, bartering, and commodity
  money — all of which are less efficient substitutes, illustrating how much productive
  capacity depends on a reliable currency.
