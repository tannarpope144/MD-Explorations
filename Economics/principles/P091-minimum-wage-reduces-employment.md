# P091 — Minimum Wage Laws Reduce Employment for the Least-Skilled

**Category:** Work & Pay
**Tags:** minimum-wage, employment, labor-market, price-floor, unintended-consequences
**Source:** Basic Economics (Sowell), Ch. 9 — Labor and Pay

## Principle
A minimum wage set above the market-clearing wage for low-skill labor is a price floor
on labor. Like any price floor, it produces a surplus: more workers willing to work at
the minimum wage than employers willing to hire at that wage. The workers most affected
are those whose productivity is below the minimum — typically the young, the
inexperienced, and those with fewer skills — who are priced out of employment entirely.

## In plain English
A minimum wage tells employers they cannot pay below a set rate. Employers respond by
hiring fewer workers, especially those whose output is worth less than the minimum.
The workers who keep their jobs get higher wages; those who lose theirs get no wages.

## The fallacy it corrects
"A higher minimum wage helps low-income workers." It helps those who keep their jobs
at the higher wage, but reduces employment and hours for those at the bottom of the
skill distribution — who are often the intended beneficiaries — through no malice
but through the basic logic of supply and demand.

## How it played out
- **Sowell's cases:** Sowell cites the disproportionate impact of minimum wage increases
  on Black teenage unemployment in the United States, where employment rates for this
  group fell sharply after minimum wage increases priced the least-experienced workers
  out of the labor market.
- **Broader cases:** Studies of minimum wage increases in U.S. cities and states
  find mixed results: modest increases in moderately tight labor markets may have
  small disemployment effects; large increases in lower-wage regions more consistently
  reduce hours and employment for low-skill workers. A widely cited study of Seattle's
  minimum wage increase found that while employment counts held up, total earnings
  of low-wage workers fell because hours were cut.
