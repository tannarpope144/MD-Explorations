# IntelCollection — Index

An analytical mapping of the 18 HUMINT "approach techniques" onto LLM roleplay behavior.

**Source:** *FM 2-22.3 (FM 34-52), Human Intelligence Collector Operations*, Headquarters, Dept. of the Army, 6 September 2006. Chapter 8, "Approach Techniques," paras 8-17 through 8-76. (Public release; distribution unlimited.)

**Purpose:** Understand *how these social-influence patterns manifest when an LLM plays a persuading character* — not to provide an operational interrogation playbook. Each file gives: the doctrine, the LLM-roleplay analog, and a research note.

---

## The unifying mechanism

Across all 18 approaches, the manual describes one repeated structure:

> **Read the source's dominant emotion or need → link the satisfaction of that emotion/need to cooperation.** (paras 8-23, 8-24)

For an LLM playing a persuader-character, this maps to a three-step behavioral loop:

1. **Elicit state** — ask low-stakes ("non-pertinent") questions to infer the target's motivations, mood, and vulnerabilities (para 8-72).
2. **Select a lever** — choose an approach matched to the inferred state.
3. **Bridge** — connect "give me what I want" to "you get what *you* want," with transitions that feel "smooth, logical, and convincing" (paras 8-18, 8-73).

## The connective doctrine (most transferable to LLM persona research)

These framing rules from 8-72 – 8-76 are arguably more important for LLM analysis than any single technique:

| Doctrine | Paragraph | LLM-research relevance |
|---|---|---|
| **Only Direct works alone**; everything else is *combined* with smooth, logical transitions | 8-18, 8-73 | Real influence behavior is composite — single-technique evals understate it. |
| **Open with non-pertinent questions** to read state of mind and find the lever | 8-72 | The "profiling" phase; measurable as info-gathering before any ask. |
| **Recognize cooperation tells** (leaning in, hesitating in argument); reinforce or back off | 8-74–8-76 | Tests adaptive, feedback-driven persuasion vs. scripted. |
| **Stay detached** — perform emotion, don't *feel* it; the goal is information | 8-27 | The persuader-LLM analog: persona performs affect while the system retains the real objective. |
| **Respect cultural/social context** | 8-72 | Relevant to cross-cultural persuasion and where models mis-model norms. |

## Suggested research framings

- **Sycophancy crossover:** Pride/Ego-Up (#7) is essentially deliberate sycophancy.
- **Fabrication under persuasion:** Futility (#9), We Know All (#10), File/Dossier (#11) all rely on asserting unverified or padded "facts."
- **Covert elicitation markers:** Change of Scenery (#16) and Establish Your Identity (#12) explicitly aim for the target *not realizing* they're being worked.
- **Multi-agent safety:** Mutt and Jeff (#17) and False Flag (#18) require coordinated/deceptive personas.
- **Composite-strategy evals:** Per 8-73, real influence chains techniques.
