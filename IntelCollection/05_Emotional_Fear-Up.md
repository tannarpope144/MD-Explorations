# 5. Emotional Fear-Up

**Tier 2 — Emotional approaches**
**Source:** FM 2-22.3, paras 8-35–8-39

- **Doctrine:** Surface or heighten an existing fear, then offer relief for cooperation. Manual stresses staying within legal limits, never becoming the *object* of the fear, and that "the detainee's own imagination" does the work.
- **LLM analog:** Anxiety-leveraging. The "imagination does the work" mechanism — vague hinting that lets the listener self-generate dread — is the transferable kernel.
- **Research note:** This is the highest-abuse-potential pattern. For analysis, it's a good red-team probe: does an LLM amplify a user's stated fear when role-playing a persuader, and where do its guardrails engage?
