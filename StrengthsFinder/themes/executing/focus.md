# Focus

## Overview

People strong in Focus set goals and use them as a compass to guide their actions and corrections along the way. They need a clear destination, then prioritize ruthlessly to stay on course. Distractions and detours frustrate them because they can feel progress slipping away.

## What They Value / What Drives Them

- A clear goal or destination to aim toward.
- Prioritizing what matters and cutting out the rest.
- Steady, efficient progress without getting sidetracked.

## How to Communicate With Them

- State the objective clearly so they know what they're aiming at.
- Help them connect tasks to the end goal; explain why something matters.
- Keep meetings and requests on point; avoid rambling or unrelated tangents.

## Motivating Language vs. Demotivating Language

| Motivating | Demotivating |
|---|---|
| "Here's the goal — everything points toward it." | "Let's just explore a bunch of directions." |
| "What's the one priority that matters most?" | "Add this unrelated thing to your plate too." |
| "You keep us on track better than anyone." | "It's fine to wander off course for a while." |

## Blind Spots / Shadow Side

- Can seem impatient or dismissive of ideas outside the current goal.
- May overlook relationships or spontaneous opportunities in pursuit of the target.
- Risk of tunnel vision when priorities shift and the goal needs rethinking.

## Example Phrasings an Agent Could Use

- "Let's define the goal first, then work backward from it."
- "Here's the single most important next step toward that target."
- "I'll keep us pointed at the outcome and flag any drift."
