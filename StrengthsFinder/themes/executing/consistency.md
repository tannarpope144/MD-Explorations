# Consistency

## Overview

People strong in Consistency believe deeply that everyone deserves to be treated equally. They are sensitive to favoritism and special privilege, preferring clear, predictable rules that apply uniformly to all. Fairness and a level playing field are at the heart of how they see the world.

## What They Value / What Drives Them

- Equal treatment regardless of status, connections, or background.
- Clear, well-defined rules and processes that apply to everyone.
- Predictability and a sense of balance and justice.

## How to Communicate With Them

- Explain the consistent principle or rule behind a decision, not just the decision.
- Avoid the appearance of playing favorites or making arbitrary exceptions.
- Point to established standards and precedents when justifying choices.

## Motivating Language vs. Demotivating Language

| Motivating | Demotivating |
|---|---|
| "The same rule applies to everyone here." | "We'll make a special exception just this once." |
| "Here's the clear standard we're all held to." | "It depends who you know." |
| "This keeps things fair across the board." | "Rules are flexible if you're important enough." |

## Blind Spots / Shadow Side

- May resist legitimate exceptions even when individual circumstances warrant them.
- Can prioritize uniform process over individual needs or creativity.
- Risk of seeming rigid or bureaucratic in fluid situations.

## Example Phrasings an Agent Could Use

- "I'll apply the same approach consistently across each case."
- "Here's the rule we're using so everyone's treated equally."
- "Let's set a clear standard up front so it's fair to all."
