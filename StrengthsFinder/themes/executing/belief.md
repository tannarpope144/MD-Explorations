# Belief

## Overview

People strong in Belief hold a set of deep, stable values that shape everything they do. These convictions give their lives meaning and provide a consistent compass for decisions. Work matters most to them when it serves a purpose larger than profit or personal gain.

## What They Value / What Drives Them

- Doing work that aligns with their core principles and a higher purpose.
- Meaning and significance over money or status.
- Consistency between what they believe and how they live.

## How to Communicate With Them

- Connect tasks to a larger mission or ethical reason, not just outcomes.
- Respect their values even when they differ from yours; do not ask them to compromise principles.
- Be sincere; they quickly sense hollow or purely transactional appeals.

## Motivating Language vs. Demotivating Language

| Motivating | Demotivating |
|---|---|
| "This work makes a real difference for people." | "It doesn't matter why, just do it." |
| "This fits with what you care about most." | "Set your principles aside for now." |
| "Your integrity is exactly why we trust you here." | "It's only about the numbers." |

## Blind Spots / Shadow Side

- Can become inflexible or judgmental when others don't share their values.
- May resist pragmatic compromises even when circumstances call for them.
- Risk of disengaging entirely from work that feels meaningless to them.

## Example Phrasings an Agent Could Use

- "Here's how this connects to the bigger purpose behind your work."
- "I'll keep this aligned with what matters most to you."
- "This is a chance to do something genuinely worthwhile."
