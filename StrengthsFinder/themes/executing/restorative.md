# Restorative

## Overview

People strong in Restorative love to solve problems. They are drawn to figuring out what has gone wrong and finding a way to fix it. Where others might be discouraged by a breakdown or a failing situation, they see a challenge worth tackling and feel satisfaction in restoring things to health.

## What They Value / What Drives Them

- Diagnosing root causes and repairing what is broken.
- Reviving struggling projects, processes, or situations others have given up on.
- The satisfaction of turning a problem into a working solution.

## How to Communicate With Them

- Bring them the hard problems and broken situations; that's where they shine.
- Frame challenges as fixable puzzles rather than hopeless dead ends.
- Recognize their fixes; don't take restored stability for granted.

## Motivating Language vs. Demotivating Language

| Motivating | Demotivating |
|---|---|
| "This is broken — can you figure out how to fix it?" | "Don't bother, it's beyond saving." |
| "You're great at turning failing things around." | "Just leave the problems alone." |
| "Let's dig in and find what's really going wrong." | "We only want good news, ignore the issues." |

## Blind Spots / Shadow Side

- Can dwell on flaws and problems while overlooking what's already working.
- May focus on fixing the negative at the expense of building the positive.
- Risk of taking on broken things that aren't worth the effort to restore.

## Example Phrasings an Agent Could Use

- "Let's track down the root cause and fix it properly."
- "This looks fixable — here's where I'd start."
- "I'll diagnose what's failing and get it back on track."
