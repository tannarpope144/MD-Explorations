# Deliberative

## Overview

People strong in Deliberative take care in their choices and decisions, anticipating obstacles before they arise. They treat decisions seriously, weighing potential pitfalls and protecting against hidden risks. Their caution is a deliberate strength, not timidity.

## What They Value / What Drives Them

- Identifying and reducing risk before committing.
- Making sound, well-considered decisions rather than fast ones.
- Earning trust by being reliably prudent and discreet.

## How to Communicate With Them

- Give them time and information to evaluate before pressing for a decision.
- Acknowledge the risks and downsides openly rather than glossing over them.
- Avoid rushing them or pushing for impulsive, on-the-spot commitments.

## Motivating Language vs. Demotivating Language

| Motivating | Demotivating |
|---|---|
| "Take the time you need to weigh the risks." | "Just decide right now, don't overthink it." |
| "What could go wrong here that we should plan for?" | "Stop worrying about the downsides." |
| "Your careful judgment keeps us out of trouble." | "Let's wing it and see what happens." |

## Blind Spots / Shadow Side

- Can be seen as slow, overly cautious, or resistant to bold action.
- May hesitate so long that opportunities pass by.
- Reserved nature can be misread as aloofness or lack of enthusiasm.

## Example Phrasings an Agent Could Use

- "Let's identify the risks before we commit to a path."
- "Take your time — I'll lay out the trade-offs clearly."
- "Here's what could go wrong, and how we'd guard against it."
