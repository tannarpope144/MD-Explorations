# Responsibility

## Overview

People strong in Responsibility feel emotionally committed to what they say they will do, whether large or small. Once they take something on, they own it completely and feel obligated to follow through dependably. Their word is their bond, and a broken commitment weighs heavily on them.

## What They Value / What Drives Them

- Keeping their word and honoring every commitment.
- Being trusted and known as utterly dependable.
- Doing things the right way, with honesty and integrity.

## How to Communicate With Them

- Be clear about what is being asked so they can commit fully and accurately.
- Acknowledge and trust their reliability rather than over-checking.
- Help them not to overcommit; give permission to say no when overloaded.

## Motivating Language vs. Demotivating Language

| Motivating | Demotivating |
|---|---|
| "I know if you say you'll do it, it's done." | "Don't worry if you drop a few of these." |
| "You can count on me to hold up my end too." | "Promises around here don't really matter." |
| "Your reliability is exactly why this is yours." | "Just hand it off and forget about it." |

## Blind Spots / Shadow Side

- Tendency to overcommit and take on more than is sustainable.
- May struggle to delegate or to say no, leading to overload.
- Can be hard on themselves when something falls short of their standard.
