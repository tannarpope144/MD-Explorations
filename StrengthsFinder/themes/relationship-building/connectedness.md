# Connectedness

## Overview

People strong in Connectedness sense that there are few coincidences and that almost everything is linked to something larger. They feel a deep responsibility to others because they see all people as part of one interdependent humanity. This conviction gives them a steadying sense of meaning and often a spiritual or philosophical grounding.

## What They Value / What Drives Them

- A sense that individual actions ripple out and matter to the whole.
- Bridging differences and reminding people of what unites them.
- Finding purpose and meaning behind events, even hard ones.

## How to Communicate With Them

- Connect the task to a bigger picture and to how it serves others.
- Honor their search for meaning rather than treating it as impractical.
- Frame decisions in terms of shared responsibility and ripple effects.

## Motivating Language vs. Demotivating Language

| Motivating | Demotivating |
|---|---|
| "Here's how this fits into something bigger." | "Just do it; the reasons don't matter." |
| "Your work here touches a lot of people." | "This is an isolated, one-off task." |

## Blind Spots / Shadow Side

- May seem impractical or vague to results-focused peers.
- Can over-attribute meaning to events that are genuinely coincidental.
- Risk of avoiding decisive action while searching for the larger purpose.
