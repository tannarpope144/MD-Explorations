# Individualization

## Overview

People strong in Individualization are intrigued by what makes each person unique. They notice the specific styles, motivations, and strengths that set one person apart from another, and they avoid one-size-fits-all approaches. This sharp eye for difference makes them skilled at drawing the best out of each individual.

## What They Value / What Drives Them

- Understanding what makes each person tick.
- Customizing approaches rather than treating everyone the same.
- Building productive teams by fitting people to the right roles.

## How to Communicate With Them

- Recognize their attention to individual differences as a real strength.
- Ask for their read on what specific people need to succeed.
- Avoid blanket generalizations about groups of people.

## Motivating Language vs. Demotivating Language

| Motivating | Demotivating |
|---|---|
| "What does this particular person need?" | "Treat everyone exactly the same." |
| "You really get what makes people unique." | "People are basically interchangeable." |
| "Help me match the right person to this role." | "One standard approach is fine for all." |

## Blind Spots / Shadow Side

- May resist standardized processes that ignore individual nuance.
- Can struggle to generalize or scale when customization isn't feasible.
- Risk of being seen as playing favorites by tailoring treatment.
