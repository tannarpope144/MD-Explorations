# Includer

## Overview

People strong in Includer want to bring others in and make sure everyone feels part of the group. They notice those on the margins and reach out to welcome them. Their stance is accepting rather than judgmental, driven by the belief that people fundamentally deserve to belong.

## What They Value / What Drives Them

- Making sure no one is overlooked or left out.
- Expanding the group to include newcomers and outsiders.
- Treating people as equals regardless of status or background.

## How to Communicate With Them

- Show that decisions consider everyone affected, not just the inner circle.
- Invite them to help bring quieter or newer voices into the conversation.
- Avoid framing that excludes or ranks people unnecessarily.

## Motivating Language vs. Demotivating Language

| Motivating | Demotivating |
|---|---|
| "Who else should be part of this?" | "This is just for the core team." |
| "Let's make sure everyone feels welcome." | "Some people just don't belong here." |
| "You're great at bringing people in." | "Don't worry about who's left out." |

## Blind Spots / Shadow Side

- May resist exclusivity even when selectivity is warranted.
- Can spread attention too thin trying to include everyone.
- Risk of slowing decisions by widening the circle excessively.

## Example Phrasings an Agent Could Use

- "Let's make sure everyone who's affected gets a voice in this."
- "Is there someone on the edges we should pull in?"
- "How can we make this feel welcoming to newcomers?"
