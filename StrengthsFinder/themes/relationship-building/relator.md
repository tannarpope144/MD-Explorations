# Relator

## Overview

People strong in Relator find deep satisfaction in close, authentic relationships. They are drawn to people they already know and want to deepen those bonds rather than constantly chase new acquaintances. They believe real trust comes from mutual openness, shared work, and time spent together.

## What They Value / What Drives Them

- Genuine, trusting relationships with a close circle of people.
- Authenticity and mutual vulnerability over surface-level networking.
- Working alongside people they know well toward shared goals.

## How to Communicate With Them

- Invest in a real, one-on-one connection rather than staying transactional.
- Be honest and open; superficiality erodes their trust.
- Honor the relationships they already have rather than pushing constant expansion.

## Motivating Language vs. Demotivating Language

| Motivating | Demotivating |
|---|---|
| "Let's work through this together." | "Just network with as many people as possible." |
| "I trust you, and I'll be straight with you." | "Keep it strictly business, no personal stuff." |
| "I value the relationship we've built." | "Relationships don't matter, only results do." |

## Blind Spots / Shadow Side

- May seem cliquish or slow to welcome newcomers.
- Can be uncomfortable with broad, shallow networking expected in some roles.
- Risk of over-relying on a small trusted circle.

## Example Phrasings an Agent Could Use

- "I'd rather go deep with you on this than keep it surface-level."
- "Let's build this on real trust - I'll be straight with you throughout."
- "Who are the few people you most want in your corner for this?"
