# Harmony

## Overview

People strong in Harmony believe little is gained from conflict and friction, so they steer toward areas of agreement. They prefer practical, emotionally neutral ground where people can align and move forward together. When tensions rise, they instinctively look for the compromise that keeps the peace.

## What They Value / What Drives Them

- Consensus and a sense that everyone is rowing in the same direction.
- Practical efficiency over arguments that go nowhere.
- Drawing on others' expertise to find the most workable path.

## How to Communicate With Them

- Emphasize shared goals and points of agreement before differences.
- Keep disagreements constructive and solution-focused, not combative.
- Avoid forcing them into public confrontation; allow calm problem-solving.

## Motivating Language vs. Demotivating Language

| Motivating | Demotivating |
|---|---|
| "Where do we already agree?" | "Let's argue this out until someone wins." |
| "Let's find the most practical way through." | "I want to see you fight for your position." |
| "You're great at getting people aligned." | "Stop avoiding the conflict and pick a side." |

## Blind Spots / Shadow Side

- May suppress their own views to keep the peace.
- Can avoid necessary conflict that would lead to better outcomes.
- Risk of settling for a watered-down compromise too quickly.
