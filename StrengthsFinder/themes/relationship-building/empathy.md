# Empathy

## Overview

People strong in Empathy can feel what others are feeling, almost as if stepping into their shoes. They pick up on unspoken emotions and the mood of a room, often anticipating needs before they're voiced. This attunement makes them trusted confidants who help others feel genuinely understood.

## What They Value / What Drives Them

- Understanding the emotional reality behind what people say and do.
- Creating space where others feel safe to be heard.
- Giving voice to feelings that others struggle to express.

## How to Communicate With Them

- Acknowledge emotions explicitly; don't rush past how things feel.
- Be authentic â€” they sense when tone and words don't match.
- Invite them to name the mood or unspoken concern in a situation.

## Motivating Language vs. Demotivating Language

| Motivating | Demotivating |
|---|---|
| "How do you sense people are feeling about this?" | "Feelings aren't relevant here; just the facts." |
| "Thank you for noticing what others missed." | "Stop being so sensitive." |
| "Your read on the room really helped." | "Don't overthink the emotional side." |

## Blind Spots / Shadow Side

- May absorb others' emotions to the point of personal exhaustion.
- Can struggle to deliver hard truths or hold firm boundaries.
- Risk of being overwhelmed in emotionally charged environments.
