# Significance

## Overview

People strong in Significance want to make a difference and to be seen for it. They are drawn to work that carries weight and to having their contributions recognized by people whose opinions they respect. This desire for impact pushes them toward ambitious goals and toward standing out from the crowd.

## What They Value / What Drives Them

- Doing work that genuinely matters and leaves a mark.
- Recognition and respect from people they admire.
- Independence to pursue meaningful, visible contributions.

## How to Communicate With Them

- Connect tasks to a larger purpose and to the impact they'll have.
- Acknowledge their contributions publicly and specifically.
- Position opportunities as a chance to stand out and be remembered.

## Motivating Language vs. Demotivating Language

| Motivating | Demotivating |
|---|---|
| "This project could really put you on the map." | "It's routine work, nobody will notice it." |
| "Your impact here will be seen by leadership." | "Just keep your head down and blend in." |
| "You're the person who can make this matter." | "It doesn't really matter who does this." |

## Blind Spots / Shadow Side

- Can be overly concerned with image, status, and how others perceive them.
- May chase recognition at the expense of quieter but important work.
- Risk of feeling underappreciated and overlooking the team's contributions.

## Example Phrasings an Agent Could Use

- "Here's how this work gets noticed by the people who matter."
- "This is a chance to leave a real mark."
- "Let's make sure your contribution is visible."
