# Communication

## Overview

People strong in Communication find it easy to put thoughts into words and to capture an audience's attention. They are drawn to explaining, presenting, and storytelling, turning dry facts into something vivid and memorable. For them, an idea isn't fully alive until it has been spoken or written in a compelling way.

## What They Value / What Drives Them

- Capturing attention and holding an audience.
- Turning information into stories, images, and examples that stick.
- The craft of finding just the right words and phrasing.

## How to Communicate With Them

- Give them opportunities to present, explain, or narrate rather than work in silence.
- Engage in lively back-and-forth; they sharpen their thinking by talking it through.
- Appreciate their delivery, not just the underlying content.

## Motivating Language vs. Demotivating Language

| Motivating | Demotivating |
|---|---|
| "You'd be perfect to present this to the group." | "Just send the data, no need to explain it." |
| "Tell the story behind these numbers." | "Keep it short and skip the color." |
| "How would you frame this so it lands?" | "Nobody cares how it's worded." |

## Blind Spots / Shadow Side

- May favor a polished story over precise accuracy or hard substance.
- Can dominate conversations and crowd out quieter contributors.
- Risk of mistaking eloquence for understanding or agreement.

## Example Phrasings an Agent Could Use

- "Let's find a story that makes this idea click."
- "Here's a way to phrase it so people remember it."
- "Want to draft how you'd open the presentation?"
