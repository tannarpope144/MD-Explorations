# Woo

## Overview

People strong in Woo (Winning Others Over) thrive on meeting new people and earning their goodwill. Striking up a conversation with a stranger feels easy and even invigorating to them, and they take satisfaction in finding common ground quickly. The thrill is in making the connection, not necessarily in deepening every one.

## What They Value / What Drives Them

- Meeting new people and expanding their circle.
- The satisfaction of breaking the ice and building instant rapport.
- Wide networks full of friendly, casual connections.

## How to Communicate With Them

- Give them chances to interact with new people and represent the group.
- Keep interactions warm, social, and energetic rather than purely transactional.
- Let them work the room; don't confine them to solitary tasks.

## Motivating Language vs. Demotivating Language

| Motivating | Demotivating |
|---|---|
| "Go introduce yourself and win them over." | "Just stay at your desk and crank through this alone." |
| "You're great at making people feel welcome." | "Skip the small talk and get to business." |
| "We need you to connect with the new crowd." | "Stick with the same few people you already know." |

## Blind Spots / Shadow Side

- May come across as superficial or insincere to those who prefer depth.
- Can collect many shallow connections while neglecting deeper relationships.
- Risk of being seen as more interested in charm than in substance.

## Example Phrasings an Agent Could Use

- "You'd be perfect to break the ice with this new group."
- "Let's get you in front of more people who should know you."
- "Who's someone new worth connecting with this week?"
