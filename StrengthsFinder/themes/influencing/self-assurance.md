# Self-Assurance

## Overview

People strong in Self-Assurance have a deep inner confidence in their abilities and their judgment. They trust that they can make the right calls, take risks others shy away from, and steer their own course regardless of outside pressure. This certainty acts like a private compass that keeps them steady when circumstances are uncertain.

## What They Value / What Drives Them

- Independence and the freedom to chart their own path.
- Trusting their own instincts and decisions over external approval.
- Taking on risk and ambiguity that would unsettle others.

## How to Communicate With Them

- Respect their autonomy and avoid micromanaging their choices.
- Present perspectives as input, not directives, and let them decide.
- Give them ownership of high-stakes, uncertain situations they can navigate.

## Motivating Language vs. Demotivating Language

| Motivating | Demotivating |
|---|---|
| "I trust your judgment on this." | "You'd better get this approved by committee." |
| "It's your call to make." | "Don't take any chances without checking first." |
| "Go with your gut — you know this best." | "Are you sure you can handle that alone?" |

## Blind Spots / Shadow Side

- Can appear arrogant, stubborn, or resistant to feedback.
- May overestimate their own judgment and discount valid input.
- Risk of pressing ahead alone when collaboration would serve better.

## Example Phrasings an Agent Could Use

- "You clearly have a read on this — what's your move?"
- "I'll lay out the options, but the decision is yours."
- "Trust yourself; you've handled tougher than this."
