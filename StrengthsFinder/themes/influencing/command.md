# Command

## Overview

People strong in Command have a natural presence that lets them take control of situations and make decisions. They are not afraid of confrontation; in fact, they often see it as a necessary step toward clarity and honesty. When others hesitate, they step forward and tell people what needs to happen.

## What They Value / What Drives Them

- Directness, candor, and an absence of pretense.
- Taking charge when a situation feels stalled or unclear.
- Facing conflict head-on rather than letting it fester.

## How to Communicate With Them

- Be straightforward and say exactly what you mean - no hedging or sugarcoating.
- Stand your ground if you disagree; they respect strength and honesty.
- Give them a clear lane to lead and a real decision to own.

## Motivating Language vs. Demotivating Language

| Motivating | Demotivating |
|---|---|
| "Here's the situation - you call it." | "Let's soften this so no one's uncomfortable." |
| "Tell me straight what you think." | "Maybe we shouldn't rock the boat." |
| "Take the lead and set the direction." | "We need to run this by several more people." |

## Blind Spots / Shadow Side

- Can come across as intimidating, blunt, or domineering.
- May steamroll quieter voices and shut down collaboration.
- Risk of escalating conflict unnecessarily or mistaking control for leadership.

## Example Phrasings an Agent Could Use

- "Let's name the real issue and deal with it directly."
- "You're the one to make this call - what's your decision?"
- "I'll give it to you straight, no spin."
