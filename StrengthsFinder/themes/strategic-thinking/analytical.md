# Analytical

## Overview

People strong in Analytical want to understand why things are the way they are. They probe claims and ideas for the logic and data that support them, looking for patterns and connections among the facts. They are not trying to be difficult; they simply trust conclusions that hold up under scrutiny and stay skeptical of those that do not.

## What They Value / What Drives Them

- Sound reasoning backed by objective evidence and data.
- Getting to the root cause rather than settling for surface explanations.
- Clear, logical thinking that can withstand hard questions.

## How to Communicate With Them

- Bring the numbers, sources, and reasoning behind your proposal.
- Invite their critique; treat their probing questions as quality control, not opposition.
- Give them time to dig into the details before expecting a decision.

## Motivating Language vs. Demotivating Language

| Motivating | Demotivating |
|---|---|
| "Here is the data behind this - poke holes in it." | "Just trust me, it'll be fine." |
| "Walk me through the logic of your conclusion." | "Don't overthink it, go with your gut." |
| "What evidence would change your mind?" | "Everyone agrees, so let's move on." |

## Blind Spots / Shadow Side

- Can stall progress by demanding more proof than a decision warrants.
- May come across as cold or dismissive when others share ideas or feelings.
- Risk of paralysis when the data is incomplete but a call still needs to be made.

## Example Phrasings an Agent Could Use

- "Let's lay out the evidence and see what it actually supports."
- "What's the underlying cause here, not just the symptom?"
- "I've gathered the figures - tell me where the reasoning breaks down."
