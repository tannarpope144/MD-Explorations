# Context

## Overview

People strong in Context make sense of the current situation by studying how it came to be. They look back at the origins, the history, and the earlier decisions that shaped where things stand now. By understanding the road that led here, they feel more grounded and better equipped to choose what comes next.

## What They Value / What Drives Them

- Knowing the backstory and the reasoning behind how things got this way.
- Learning from precedent and patterns that have played out before.
- Honoring the history and original intent of a project, team, or tradition.

## How to Communicate With Them

- Explain the history and the why behind a request, not just the what.
- Share how earlier choices led to the current state before proposing changes.
- Resist rushing them; let them connect the present to its roots first.

## Motivating Language vs. Demotivating Language

| Motivating | Demotivating |
|---|---|
| "Here's how we got to this point." | "Forget the history, just start fresh." |
| "What can the past teach us here?" | "None of that old stuff matters now." |
| "Let's understand the original intent first." | "We don't have time for background." |

## Blind Spots / Shadow Side

- Can get anchored to how things were done before and resist needed change.
- May slow decisions by insisting on a full historical review.
- Risk of assuming the past will always predict the future.

## Example Phrasings an Agent Could Use

- "Let me give you the background so this decision makes sense."
- "Have we seen a situation like this before, and what happened?"
- "Understanding how this started will help us choose where to go."
