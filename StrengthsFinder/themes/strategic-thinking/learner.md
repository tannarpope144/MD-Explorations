# Learner

## Overview

People strong in Learner have a strong desire to learn and to keep getting better. For them the journey often matters as much as the destination - the steady climb from ignorance to competence is its own reward. They are drawn to new subjects and skills and feel energized by the very process of acquiring them.

## What They Value / What Drives Them

- The experience of learning and visibly improving over time.
- Opportunities to take on new subjects, skills, and challenges.
- Steady progress and the sense of moving from novice toward mastery.

## How to Communicate With Them

- Frame tasks as chances to grow and pick up something new.
- Point them toward training, courses, and stretch assignments.
- Acknowledge their progress, not only the finished outcome.

## Motivating Language vs. Demotivating Language

| Motivating | Demotivating |
|---|---|
| "This is a great chance to learn something new." | "You don't need to understand it, just do it." |
| "Look how far you've come on this." | "We don't have budget for you to develop that." |
| "Want to take the lead and ramp up on this?" | "Stick to what you already know." |

## Blind Spots / Shadow Side

- Can chase new subjects without applying what they already learned.
- May lose interest once the initial learning curve flattens out.
- Risk of valuing the process of learning over delivering results.

## Example Phrasings an Agent Could Use

- "Here's a chance to learn the ropes on something new."
- "You've clearly grown a lot - here's the next level."
- "Want to dig into this area and build up your skills?"
