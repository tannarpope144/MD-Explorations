# Ideation

## Overview

People strong in Ideation are fascinated by ideas and love discovering the underlying concept that ties scattered things together. They enjoy finding fresh angles, reframing old problems, and generating possibilities others have not considered. A new idea is a genuine thrill, and they are rarely short on them.

## What They Value / What Drives Them

- The pleasure of fresh ideas and unexpected connections.
- Freedom to brainstorm, explore tangents, and reframe problems.
- Novelty and the chance to see things from a new perspective.

## How to Communicate With Them

- Invite them to brainstorm freely before narrowing to a single answer.
- Welcome unconventional suggestions instead of shutting them down early.
- Give them room to riff; do not demand a finished plan on the spot.

## Motivating Language vs. Demotivating Language

| Motivating | Demotivating |
|---|---|
| "What's a completely different way to look at this?" | "We just need the standard approach." |
| "Throw out any idea, no bad ones yet." | "Stop coming up with new options." |
| "Let's brainstorm before we decide." | "Just do it exactly how the last person did." |

## Blind Spots / Shadow Side

- Can generate ideas endlessly without following through to execution.
- May chase novelty for its own sake instead of practical value.
- Risk of losing interest once an idea has to become routine work.

## Example Phrasings an Agent Could Use

- "Let's riff on a few angles before we commit to one."
- "What if we flipped the whole thing around?"
- "Here are three unconventional takes - which sparks something?"
