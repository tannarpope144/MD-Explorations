# Strategic

## Overview

People strong in Strategic can quickly see patterns where others see complexity, then chart a clear way through. They naturally generate alternative routes toward a goal and sort the promising paths from the dead ends. This is less about deliberate analysis and more about an instinct for spotting the option most likely to work.

## What They Value / What Drives Them

- Seeing the multiple paths available and selecting the most promising one.
- Anticipating obstacles and planning around them before they hit.
- Cutting through complexity to find a clear, workable route to the goal.

## How to Communicate With Them

- Lay out the goal and let them map the routes to reach it.
- Ask which path they'd choose and why before locking in a plan.
- Invite them to anticipate what could go wrong and how to avoid it.

## Motivating Language vs. Demotivating Language

| Motivating | Demotivating |
|---|---|
| "What's the best path to get us there?" | "Just follow the steps, don't question them." |
| "Map out a few options and pick the smartest one." | "There's only one way to do this." |
| "What could trip us up, and how do we steer around it?" | "Don't worry about what's ahead." |

## Blind Spots / Shadow Side

- Can struggle to explain the instinct behind a choice others want justified.
- May grow impatient when forced down a single predetermined path.
- Risk of moving on to the next route before the current one is finished.

## Example Phrasings an Agent Could Use

- "Here are a few ways to get there - which feels strongest?"
- "Let's anticipate the obstacles before we commit."
- "What's the smartest route given where we want to end up?"
