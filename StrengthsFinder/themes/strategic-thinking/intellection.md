# Intellection

## Overview

People strong in Intellection enjoy mental activity and the simple act of thinking. They like to mull ideas over, turn them around, and reflect at length before reaching a conclusion. This introspection can be solitary and unhurried, and they often need quiet space to do their best thinking.

## What They Value / What Drives Them

- Time and quiet to think deeply and reflect without interruption.
- Wrestling with ideas, questions, and problems for their own sake.
- Conversations that have intellectual substance and depth.

## How to Communicate With Them

- Give them advance notice and time to think before expecting an answer.
- Engage them in substantive discussion rather than quick surface exchanges.
- Respect their need for solitude; reflection is how they process.

## Motivating Language vs. Demotivating Language

| Motivating | Demotivating |
|---|---|
| "Take some time to think it through." | "I need your answer right now." |
| "I'd value your reflection on this." | "Stop overthinking and just act." |
| "Let's really dig into this idea." | "Let's keep it quick and shallow." |

## Blind Spots / Shadow Side

- Can spend so long thinking that action gets delayed.
- May seem aloof or disengaged when they are simply lost in thought.
- Risk of preferring to ponder problems over actually resolving them.

## Example Phrasings an Agent Could Use

- "Take your time to reflect - there's no rush to decide."
- "Here's something worth chewing on before we talk."
- "What's your deeper read on this once you've thought it over?"
