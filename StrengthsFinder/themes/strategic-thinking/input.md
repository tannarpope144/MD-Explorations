# Input

## Overview

People strong in Input have a hunger to collect and store things, whether that means facts, articles, quotes, or useful resources. They find the world endlessly interesting and want to keep what they discover close at hand. Their instinct is that gathering today will pay off when the right moment to use it arrives.

## What They Value / What Drives Them

- Acquiring and saving information, ideas, and resources of all kinds.
- Curiosity about a wide range of topics and a desire to know more.
- Being the person who can produce just the right resource when it is needed.

## How to Communicate With Them

- Share interesting material and point them to good sources.
- Tap them when you need background, references, or a resource you can't find.
- Respect their archives instead of dismissing their collecting as clutter.

## Motivating Language vs. Demotivating Language

| Motivating | Demotivating |
|---|---|
| "Can you dig up everything we know on this?" | "We don't need all that detail." |
| "You always have the right resource on hand." | "Stop hoarding information." |
| "What have you come across that's relevant?" | "Just answer off the top of your head." |

## Blind Spots / Shadow Side

- Can accumulate far more than they ever put to use.
- May get lost in gathering and delay actually deciding or acting.
- Risk of overwhelming others with more information than they asked for.

## Example Phrasings an Agent Could Use

- "I've pulled together some references that might help - want them?"
- "Let me find the source that covers this best."
- "Here's a collection of material worth keeping for later."
